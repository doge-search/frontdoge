Question:

What is the reputation of the computer science PhD program at the CUNY Graduate Center?
Answer:
Based on research, CUNY Graduate Center is ranked 82nd in the nation, see CUNY--Graduate Center.
Answer:
The ranking was around 50th when I started at this program. No surprise that it fell to 82nd.

There were a few professors that published on research that I was passionate about. It also had cheap(er) tuition, and I planned to pay out of pocket while working. I regretted my decision more and more every year. I ended up hating the research I used to love.

The program has most of its faculty members scattered around CUNY colleges all over New York City such as Hunter College, Baruch College,Queens College, Staten Island, John Jay and Brooklyn College. It is hard to put together a committee of professors who know each other and can work together well. Most professors do not do solid research;with no grant money,they do not care about graduating students. You will have to travel couple hours to meet with your advisor because he/she generally will not come to Manhattan to meet with you.

And the worse part is that their scholarships suck, you need to do (ADJUNCT!) teaching that means traveling to those far away CUNY colleges couple times a week. If you are young and appreciate the real teaching experience, then you are going to love it.But that means less time for your research.

There is also this thing called RFCUNY which works like a consulting company. They market the services of CS students with good programing skills to off-campus locations and pay them an hourly rate only $12 in return. The work is not even remotely related to research to be of use to the student either. Absolutely horrible for especially international students because they will not have many off campus options to support themselves otherwise.

Few professors have grant money and they pay a more decent wage ($20+/hour) but they have to pay through RFCUNY, which takes a portion of the professor's grant money leaving no money for the professor to offer health benefits to the student. While you work 40+ hours, most of the times, you can only get paid for 19 hours/week. Otherwise, they would have to pay for your benefits.

In this program, the average time it takes to graduate is much longer. I suspect that the graduation rate is not high either. If you care about your health--both mentally and physically, I recommend you look somewhere else. Otherwise, this program will be an ultimate test of your endurance. Most importantly, forget about getting married and especially having kids while you are in this program. If you will attempt this make sure you pick a financially stable spouse. A significant number of my classmates had to drop out due to this issue.

On paper this school is not prestigious, but if you survive this, you can survive just about anything!

Finally, I see that they revamped their page with tons of buzzwords hoping to attract "better" students. Unless they understand that the issue was not the student "quality", good luck trying to improve the reputation of this program.
Answer:
I know a few people in their EE PhD program. They are quite contended with the program. I guess you can search out their current students or alumni to check in more detail


Question:

What are some great restaurants or spots near the CUNY Graduate Center that are worth checking out as a student?
Answer:
Your best bets are in Koreatown (W. 31st through 33rd St., btw. 5th and 6th Ave.) and the food trucks in Herald Square.

I don't know if you've been to Koreatown yet. In case you haven't--Koreatown is bigger than it might seem on the map, because there's lots of vertical stacking.

When I was taking classes at the GC, other people in my cohort and I would sometimes go to the Food Gallery. It's cheap, it's tasty, and there's a good variety of Korean, other East Asian, and Asian fusion cuisines.

Other economical options are Woorijip and the Korean/French bakeries (Paris Baguette and Tous les Jours).

For a special celebratory meal, I recommend one of the Korean barbecue places. As far as I can tell, none of them is bad. They aren't exactly grad student stipend cheap, though.

I suspect you can't go wrong with any of the bbq places in the neighborhood, but I remember some great meals at Madang Sui and Kang Suh. (Shilla gets great reviews, but I don't think I've been there.)
