Vanderbilt University

编辑



Vanderbilt University (also known informally as Vandy) is a private research university located in Nashville, Tennessee, founded in 1873. It was named in honor of shipping and rail magnate Cornelius Vanderbilt, who provided the school its initial $1 million endowment despite having never been to the South. Vanderbilt hoped that his gift and the greater work of the university would help to heal the sectional wounds inflicted by the Civil War.
Today, Vanderbilt enrolls approximately 12,000 students from all 50 U.S. states and over 90 foreign countries in four undergraduate and six graduate and professional schools. Several research centers and institutes are affiliated with the university, including the Vanderbilt Institute for Public Policy Studies, Freedom Forum First Amendment Center, Dyer Observatory, and Vanderbilt University Medical Center, the only Level I trauma center in Middle Tennessee. With the exception of the off-campus observatory and satellite medical clinics, all of the university's facilities are situated on its 330-acre (1.3 km2) campus in the heart of Nashville, 1.5 miles (2.4 km) from downtown. Despite its urban surroundings, the campus itself is a national arboretum and features over 300 different species of trees and shrubs.

青藤导师 | 王潇在Peabody College，Vanderbilt University的一段故事
大家好！我是杨小编。今天由我来做访谈。王小编呢？ 今天他的角色是被访者。哈哈！
先介绍下王小编，他是从本科西安电子科技大学物理系，跨专业申请到了美国教育排名第一的范德堡大学Peabody教育学院。他到底是什么背景，让他在这么激烈的竞争中脱颖而出呢？让我们一起来探索吧！

杨小编：你是如何选择你的留学学校和专业的？
我就读的是美国田纳西州纳什维尔（Nashville）的范德堡大学，国际教育政策及管理专业。申请这所学校的原因是——它跟杜克大学，莱斯大学等等都被称（或者“自称”）为是“南方哈佛”。开玩笑的。其实我申请的时候压根就没听说过“田纳西”，“范德堡大学”，或者是“南方哈佛”的名号。我当时确认要申请教育学，去US News教育学排行榜查学校——当时范德堡大学的教育学院（Peabody College）排名第一。作为一个普通的重视排名的中国学生，我自然就申请了这所我毫无了解的“梦校”。
（这是王潇从本科西安电子科技大学毕业时候的样子，真是一个happy person啊！）
至于专业的选择，说起来倒是蛮有意思的。最开始我选择的是高等教育管理专业（Higher Education Administration）。不过在正式申请之前，我发了邮件跟几名教授“套瓷”。其中歪打误撞，联系到了International Educational Policy and Management（以下简称IEPM）的director， Stephen Heyneman。他建议我说，“你没有工作经验，申请高等教育管理的专业可够呛。何不来试试我们国际教育政策的专业呢？”我一听教授都这么说，那我还扭捏什么？再加上Vanderbilt University不收取任何申请费（绝对良心学校！）。我就一个字——申！
（这就是王潇在2015年冬天回到Peabody College， 在曾经就读院系的教学楼Payne Hall楼前的自拍， ）
杨小编：你觉得在申请过程中，是自己哪方面的能力在竞争中胜出，最终获得了录取？
首先，我觉得上面提到的套瓷对我帮助十分大。我甚至觉得占了我被录取因素的70％。当然，这并不是建议每个申请者就应该拿出70%的时间去不停地给目标学校的教授写信。因为另外30%的内容可能才是根本原因。这30%当然指的是个人的能力和潜力了。毕竟对于申请评审委员会来说，录取的标准和过程是绝对保密的。但我猜测（其实我很自信地说）我有三个方面的能力吸引了申请评审委员会的成员们。
（Peabody教育学院的标志性建筑Wyatt Center）
第一，在大学期间，我进行了很多商业实践——从大一一开学的卖几十块的电话卡，到大二时候组队自筹了几千块钱去卖台灯插线板，再到后来跑到校外去开茶楼等一系列活动；多多少少证明自己还有些商业头脑和学习实践能力（请注意，我申请的是教育“政策”专业，对于数字和经济的敏感是很重要的）。
第二，本科四年我还参加了很多的社会实践工作，包括最常见的支教，班级活动，还有2008年四川地震过后的心理重建、图书馆重建工作；教育学（或者是任何的社会学科）自然非常看重申请者对于社会工作的参与程度和认识；
第三，我觉得也是最为重要的一点——我对教育的热忱。基本上我就证明了一点，我知道教育学又辛苦，又不挣钱，但我仍然愿意付去一生去追寻。老师看我这么执着，也不好拒绝我了吧？呵呵。
在此，我还要特别借这个难得的机会，感谢我在西安电子科大的学长薛喻文。是他在我大三确定有出国想法之后，给我上了一门“出国申请101”——让我对于留学申请的整个过程有了正确的认识。并且他非常无私地把自己的所有英语准备资料，还有自己的申请资料全部分享给了我。他的指引，让我无比受用！我感到非常的幸运。他一直是我学习的标杆！谢谢喻文学长！
（2012年与薛喻文学长第一次在美国相聚与宾夕法尼亚大学的学生宿舍。当时喻文学长正要启程前往美国硅谷工作。）

范德堡大学经常被称为“南方哈佛”
范德堡大学（Vanderbilt University），简称Vandy，又名范德比尔特大学，由美国铁路大亨科尼利尔斯·范德比尔特捐建，是位于美国田纳西州纳什维尔市的一所著名的国家级私立研究型大学。学校创立于1873年，是位于美国南方的顶级名校，也是世界一流大学之一，属于25所新常春藤盟校。2016年范德堡大学位列US News全美大学综合排名第15位，以其出色的教育质量和较强的科研实力，享有“南方哈佛”（Harvard of the South）的美誉。
作为一所精英学府，范德堡大学拥有11个学院，并在商、法、医、教四大学术领域稳居北美一流行列，享有南方第一贵族名门的盛誉。教育学院全美顶尖。法学院为美国法学院中的老牌劲旅，依赖校友会与一些显赫的商业财团和华尔街最有权势的律师事务所保持密切的关系。医学院为中田纳西唯一的一级外伤中心。范德堡大学体育设施完备，棒球队，篮球队和橄榄球队在全美大学联赛中名列前茅。
学校景色优美，拥有330英亩森林，1988年被认定为国家植物园，拥有300种以上的植物。学校中心的Peabody College被列为国家历史古迹。主校区艺术雕塑遍布，拥有哥特式风格建筑及砖瓦玻璃的现代建筑。

Question:

What is it like to pursue an MS in computer science at Vanderbilt University? How are the intern/co-op/employment opportunities?
Answer:
Hello, I read your post. I am gonna be honest with you, I am from Tennessee, its the state I was born in. With that said Vanderbilt's reputation in this area, is stronger than Harvard's. Now that says a lot about Vanderbilt, and the brethren, of the state I call home. One of my cousins attended Vanderbilt law school, and another got her degree from Vandy in Biomedical Engineering.

Now that I have built all that up about Vanderbilt, I personally looked at many different graduate programs for a masters in computer science: Stanford, CMU, Baylor, Berkley, GT, Texas (because a friend of mine from undergrad got into a PhD program in Computational Chemistry), Cal Tech, V. Tech, and few other schools.

Long story made short, Vanderbilt was not at the top of my list, and its by far the most prestigious school in my state. The computer science department doesn't match up to some of the other programs they have there. Had you asked about Med School, or Law School, the answer without a doubt, would have been yes its an excellent program. Don't get me wrong, Vandy is still ranked in the top 75 M.S.C.S. schools but for spending ~100 to 125 grand (tuition and living expenses) on a Masters in Comp. Sci., you could get into a better school (ranking wise). Meaning a better return on investment. Also 125, over a two year span at Vanderbilt and in Nashville, thats probably average. Basically you would be an extremely in debt, broke college student.

I am not exaggerating either. I actually wrote a couple of statistical programs to evaluate over two dozen M.S.C.S. programs I looked at. To be honest though, I really only wanted to attend about a half dozen those schools. The programs I wrote to compare the M.S.C.S. programs took factors of tuition, cost of living in that locale, if the university offered an online program, the schools MSCS ranking (according to U.S. news and report), the schools ranking as far programming, GRE score, research ops, and possible return on investment. The output of the programs basically looked like this:

BEST ALL-AROUND: Georgia Tech

G.T. had the lowest annual tuition, by at least 10,000. In some cases, GT cost 30 grand less annually (not including living expenses). Atl can be expensive, but no where near as expensive as the North East.
Average GRE score 317.
Always a top 15 school in Comp Sci, usually a top 10.

NOTABLE RUNNER UP, BEST ALL-AROUND:
(as a non-resident or out of state student)
Berkeley // Every heard BSD?? Lots of history!
You could possibly grind out a MSCS for less than 75 grand, with no stipends or grants. Research is big at Berkeley (and every other school on the list)

OTHER NOTABLE UNIVERSITIES RANKED HIGH ALL AROUND:
Cal Tech. // former students Adam D'Angelo and many others
Illinois Urbana-Champaign // former students Larry Ellison, Chris Lattner

NOTE: The next category, I created. I like to call it the 100 grander. Meaning it would take more than 100 grand to complete a MS in Comp. Sci.

THE HUNDRED GRANDERS:
Standford // top 5 perennial comp sci program
Harvard // top 10 perennial comp sci program at the Graduate level
CMU // top 5 perennial comp sci program, ~50 grand or more tuition/fees/books (not including living expenses in Pittsburgh)

All of which had tuitions well over 30 grand a year, not including living expenses (room and board) and transportation cost.

It should be noted, that for 125 grand at CMU you would end being hall squatter annually. It would take at least 160 over course of the two years.

If your gonna spend 100 grand (or less) over two years the schools rank like this:
1) GT
2) Berkeley
3) Cal Tech
4) Illinois Urbana-Champaign

If "money ain't thing", which is not the case on your behalf, or you would not be asking about intern/co-op/employment ops; CMU, Stanford, and Harvard tend to yield the highest return on your investment. I think a lot of that has to do with alumni and Department/University contacts.

Back to the point of the matter of Vandy, you would get a great education, at one of the most prestigious universities in the country. As far as universities in the southeast: Vandy, Duke, Emory, Wake Forest, Clemson and GT are by far the most prestigious. Vandy is a perennial top 25 school in the U.S., in many areas of study, and if you do attend there; you will be around some of the greatest minds, all around, that reside in this country. There is also some very good looking people who go to school there. I'm throwing that out there as fringe benefit.

I hope this helps!
P.S. If i gonna spend as much 125 total, I would go to Standford or Harvard as a day student. Although Harvard slipped to #18 this year in the rankings of MSCS.

P.S.S. I think you could go to M.I.T. for that kind of money (125 grand for two years), if your GRE score was around 330.
