Naval Postgraduate School

编辑


The Naval Postgraduate School (NPS) is a fully accredited masters-level university operated by the United States Navy. Located in Monterey, California, it grants master's degrees, engineer's degrees and doctoral degrees. The school also offers research fellowship opportunities at the postdoctoral level through the National Research Council research associateship program.

quora评论


Question:

What is it like to attend the US Naval Postgraduate School?
Answer:
I went there as a Naval officer from 1998 to 2000. Monterey is a beautiful city, and the NPS campus is also very nice. I studied Modelling, Virtual Environments and Simulation (computer science-type stuff). The courses were certainly challenging, and they took into consideration that students there may be a few years out of school. Most officers are commissioned with Bachelor's degrees from their undergrad college, and then serve for four to eight years before going to Monterey, unlike most colleges where a student will often times stay at their undergrad college to get their advanced degree. Similarly, many officers will choose a master program not necessarily related to their undergrad degree. In my case, my undergrad degree is in Mechanical Engineering.

I suspect that actually attending class there is a lot like attending college anywhere. Except most of the students are active-duty military officers. The officers are highly motivated and have high expectations, and the school delivers! I would certainly encourage any active duty officers to consider NPS as a shore duty assignment (Yes, going to the school is a shore duty assignment--we get paid to go there!)
