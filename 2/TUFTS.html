Tufts University

编辑



Tufts University is a private research university incorporated in the municipality of Medford, Massachusetts. The university is organized into ten schools, including two undergraduate programs and eight graduate divisions, on four campuses in Massachusetts and the French Alps. The university emphasizes active citizenship and public service in all of its disciplines and is known for its internationalism and study abroad programs. Among its schools is the United States' oldest graduate school of international relations, the Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy.
Tufts College was founded in 1852 by Christian Universalists who worked for years to open a non-sectarian institution of higher learning. Charles Tufts donated the land for the campus on Walnut Hill, the highest point in Medford, saying that he wanted to set a "light on the hill." The name was changed to Tufts University in 1954, although the corporate name remains "the Trustees of Tufts College." For more than a century, Tufts was a small New England liberal arts college. The French American nutritionist and former professor at the Harvard School of Public Health Jean Mayer became president of Tufts in the late 1970s and, through a series of rapid acquisitions, transformed the school into a larger research university. Tufts is consistently ranked by U.S. News & World Report as the third best university in Massachusetts after Harvard and MIT and among the top 30 in the U.S.

学校天天都很萌。
学习方面么……主流是文理学院的那些啦。应该很多人都听说过我们学校的 international relationship 国际关系专业。《纸牌屋》的原作者就是从 Tufts 的 The Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy 出去的。学校虽然人少但国际化程度很高，小伙伴里有津巴布韦的帅气黑哥，有眼神迷乱的土耳其学霸，有阿尔巴尼亚小伙，有巴基斯坦大学神。本科的话，每一届大概有二十多个中国学生，不算太多。
学校的 study abroad program 非常好，和牛津大学、香港大学、浙江大学，还有德国、法国等地的高校都有交流项目。据统计百分之四十以上的学生大三都是在海外度过。
学校的兽医学院也相当棒，在更西边的 Grafton 镇子上，根据去过的小伙伴说骑自行车大概四十分钟。Facebook 主页放送大量萌萌哒小动物，还给小长颈鹿喂奶，给小狐狼安家之类的。所以我们本科的的 pre-vet program 兽医学预科也很强。
本科的 Pre-Med 医学预科也非常不错，毕竟 Tufts 的 Medical School 口碑还是相当好的。波士顿中国城那里有一栋很高的楼就是。在那附近我曾经搜到过一个 Wifi，其命名体现了刻苦学习的医学院学生对学校无尽的热爱。
我的小伙伴们兴趣都比较混搭，有修艺术史和数学双专业的，有修法语和数学双专业的，有学peace and justice 上着 C++ 的，有上的 Studio Arts 的课数量跟专业课差不多的码农，还有学 EE但常年蹲守 finance group 的当老板的全奖熊孩子。
Tufts 提供了很多很多可能性。
开销的话......学费算是比较贵的，毕竟私立大学，一年下来算上住宿费伙食费什么的大约六万多美金。但是 Tufts 学校里也提供丰富的打工赚钱的机会，食堂、图书馆、mail service 和 office of residential life 是比较主流的，起薪一般都是一小时八美元。学术方面高级一些的还可以做TA或者tutor或者grader，都有钱赚。
因为距离 Boston 市区很近，附近的娱乐活动不少，只要你乐意还是很能花得出去钱的。这些因人而异就不具体说啦。答主略宅，social 少，比较喜欢去 Park Street 看电影，一场大概12刀。周三晚上凭学生证减1刀。票价跟国内差不多。

塔夫茨大学简介
塔夫茨大学(Tufts University)是美国著名大学，也是波士顿仅次于哈佛大学和麻省理工学院的波士顿五大名校（哈佛大学、麻省理工学院、塔夫茨大学、波士顿学院，布兰迪斯大学、）之一，也是25所“新常春藤”成员之一。从学生录取、教师的教学到学术研究、教学和科研设施的建立以及对教师的业绩评估等，塔夫茨大学Tufts University都遵从以质取胜、注重革新的办学原则。塔夫茨大学同时注重学科在公共服务中的价值，并以良好的国际化和海外留学项目而闻名，并在德国、智利、中国和加纳等国设有海外中心。塔夫茨大学拥有美国最古老和最富盛名的国际关系研究生院之一，弗莱彻法律与外交学院。著名的生物医学研究中心－－塔夫茨新英格兰医学中心是塔夫茨大学医学院的主要教学医院。杰拉尔德和弗里德曼营养科学与政策学院在营养学上也是久负盛名。塔夫茨大学在2016年《美国新闻与世界报道》杂志的美国大学综合排名中位居第27名[1] ，在2015年美国CollegeFactual全美大学综合排名第39位。

Question:

Tufts University: What are the differences in being a computer science major in the School of Arts and Sciences vs School of Engineering?
Answer:
You have a lot more freedom doing a B.S. through A&S. Base requirements of the engineering school will fill out almost the entirety of your schedule outside of core CS. If you are interested in a side project, research, or a double major (plug: you can easily double in CS and cognitive science and it's an awesome program!), I'd recommend A&S. The engineering school will have you doing a lot of general, foundational engineering coursework such as physics modeling, but will also give you a better understanding of the circuitry and hardware (EE) that your CS path is built on (literally).
Answer:
In my four years at Tufts, I found only two major differences. I'll give you the short of it because a large portion of this answer requires a personal conversation.

Flexibility/Requirements: Mathematically, you're required to take more courses for your major in Engineering than Arts and Sciences (38 credits for Engineering and 34 for A+S I think). This leaves less room for another major, or minor. Additionally for Engineers, a larger portion of your credits are specific courses that are core requirements for Engineering. For A+S, you have more electives, or flexibility to choose which courses fit your requirements.

Social: This ties into the Requirements point. Since, as an Engineer, you're required to take those courses along with other Engineers, you spend a lot of time with Engineers. You do your homework with other Engineers, you eat with other Engineers.. you get the picture. That being said, if you're involved with a group outside academics (imagine that!) it's not difficult at all to offset the social silos.

That's really it, you get access to the same teachers, take a lot of classes together, work on projects together, etc. This topic is often talked about, but it's really just preference.
Answer:
Mostly the requirements.

You can do a BA and a BS through the school of Liberal Arts, and you need complete the LA requirements (English, 2nd language, etc.). A BS has a larger credit requirement than the BA if I recall correctly.

You can also do a BS through Engineering, which means you need to complete the Eng requirements (more credits, physics, chem, etc...) and not the LA requirements.

I did CS at Tufts through the Engineering School, and even though I loved it if I were to do it again I'd do a BS through Liberal Arts. For me it would have made sense since I took SMFA, Music, and German classes. Generally I also think its better since you'll have more freedom to take things you want to take (including Eng courses) and less likely to take stupid weedout courses like Chem and Physics.

And don't take MATH / COMP 22 through the Math department. You're welcome.


Question:

Which of the following schools has the best undergraduate Computer Science program: Tufts University, Northeastern University, or Boston University?
I am interested to know which of those schools has the best undergraduate CS program. Please comment with details or answers as to why one has a stronger program than the others.
Answer:
My "gut belief" is that of those three colleges in the greater Boston area, Northeastern is going to have the best program (a close friend's son just graduated from Northeastern with a BS and MS in Computer Science).

Why? Because of the Co-Op nature of Northeastern. One takes five years to complete a bachelors degree, but in that time period, a student gets three six-month Co-Op jobs in the industry. That is eighteen months of practical experience. And it was easy for my friend's son to expand that and get a Masters degree in the same time period. He was turning away great job offers. He even spent one of the six month Co-Op sessions in Italy, while working remotely for a company in the USA.

I have another friend whose son is at Tufts and working on robotics with some internship experience at iRobot and I have known others at BU.

However, the Northeastern Co-Op program, hands down, makes that the winner.
