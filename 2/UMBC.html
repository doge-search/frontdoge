University of Maryland Baltimore County

编辑



The University of Maryland, Baltimore County (often referred to as UMBC) is an American public research university, located in Baltimore County, Maryland, United States, mostly in the community of Catonsville, approximately 10 minutes (8.3 miles) from downtown Baltimore City and 30 minutes (33.5 miles) from Washington, D.C. With a fall 2014 enrollment of about 14,000 students, over 50 undergraduate majors, over 60 graduate programs, and the first university research park in Maryland, UMBC has been named the #1 Up-and-Coming University for six years in a row, since 2009, by US News & World Report.[not in citation given] In addition, US News & World Report has placed UMBC in the top ten for best undergraduate teaching six years in a row, being placed at #5, the second highest-ranked public university.[not in citation given]
Established as a part of the University of Maryland System in 1966, the university specializes in the natural sciences and engineering, while also offering programs in the liberal arts, and social sciences. Athletically, the UMBC Retrievers have 19 NCAA Division I teams that participate in the America East Conference.

Question:

Why did Balaji Viswanathan pursue his masters in Computer Science from University of Maryland, Baltimore County? Would he recommend the University to others?
Answer:
A variety of reasons:
I was working in a couple of subfields under AI and UMBC had a couple of very good professors in those subfields.
UMBC was right next to Washington DC and also relatively close to other east coast cities like the NYC. This was important both from a research perspective and a career perspective. For instance, when I finally decided to intern and not proceed with an RA for summer, I landed an interview in 2 days and good internship by the end of the week. The location did help. I also wanted to stay in close touch with the political happenings in DC and was a part of a political action group that gave me a lot of inside view of Indo-US political relationships.
I got a generous scholarship from UMBC within a few weeks of my application. I had a Teaching Assistanceship that paid both my tuition as well as provided a nice living allowance. I didn't have any money to pay for a tuition or even airfare and thus I was happy to get what I could. At that time funding was quite rare as US economy was just bouncing back from the 2001 recession.
I was in a hurry in my life back then. I didn't want to waste any time in applications or working before grad school. Thus, the first good school that admitted me was awesome. In retrospect, I wish I had worked in a non-typical career [say volunteering or in a research lab] before I went into gradschool, providing me time and perspective to apply.
Finally, when I was 20, I didn't have a very realistic plan. I was a dreamer. Thus, I applied for the three top schools in my area for Phd (MIT, Stanford and Princeton). Luckily one professor close to me also suggested UMBC for an MS. Thus, I applied for it. I knew no one who did MS in the US back then. With very little guidance from seniors, no social media and not as much content in the Internet back then, the planning was not good. In retrospect, it was 3 stretch schools and 1 safe school with no match schools in between.

In any case, make a decision with your life's priorities. And realize that you get to work with limited data. UMBC is good for a lot of career types and research fields and if that fits your goals, then go for it.
