University of Georgia

编辑



The University of Georgia, founded in 1785, and commonly referred to as UGA or simply Georgia, is an American public land-grant and sea grant research university. Its primary location is a 762-acre (3.08 km2) campus in the college town of Athens, Georgia, approximately an hour's drive from Atlanta, Georgia. It is considered the State of Georgia's flagship university. The university is ranked 21st overall among all public national universities in the current U.S. News & World Report rankings, and consistently ranks as high as 101st among all the top international universities in the world. While having high overall ratings, Georgia ranks in the top 10 for evaluations in several fields of study. The university is a part of the University System of Georgia and is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools. It is classified as a "Research University / Very High Research Activity" with a "More Selective" student body by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education.
Founded in 1785 as the United States' first state-chartered university, it is the oldest and second largest of Georgia's institutions of higher learning and along with the College of William and Mary and the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill claims the title of the oldest public university in the United States. The university's historic North Campus is on the U.S. National Register of Historic Places as a designated historic district, and it and the contiguous campus areas include rolling hills and extensive arboreta.
The university offers over 140 degree programs in a wide array of disciplines. Consisting of thirteen libraries spread across multiple campuses, the UGA Libraries contain a total of 4.7 million volumes and one of the nation's largest map collections. The University of Georgia is one of 126 member institutions that comprise the Association of Research Libraries.
The University of Georgia is organized into seventeen schools and colleges. The university has three primary campuses. The largest one is the main campus in Athens that includes 389 buildings, while the two others are located in Tifton, Georgia and Griffin, Georgia. The University of Georgia also has two satellite campuses located in Atlanta and Lawrenceville, Georgia. The university operates several service and outreach stations spread across the state. The total acreage utilized by the university located in 30 Georgia counties is 39,950 acres (161.672 km2).
The University of Georgia also owns a residential and research center in Washington, DC, plus three international residential and research centers located at Oxford University in Oxford, England, at Cortona, Italy, and at Monteverde, Costa Rica.
Student athletics, both varsity and intramural, are an integral part of student and alumni life. The University of Georgia's intercollegiate sports teams, commonly known by their "Georgia Bulldogs" nickname, compete in National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) Division I and the Southeastern Conference (SEC). UGA served as a founding member of the SEC in 1932. In their 121-year history, the university's varsity sports teams have won 39 national championships and 130 conference championships. The Georgia Redcoat Marching Band, the official marching band of the university, plays at sports and other events.

Question:

What are the best reasons to attend the University of Georgia?
Answer:
Let me start off by saying that this a very subjective question, and everyone answers it differently. I am currently a student at UGA and when I was looking at schools my top 3 schools were UGA, Tufts, and Berkeley. I considered 3 main factors when I was looking at schools:

1. Environment- Wherever I went to school I wanted it to feel welcome and inviting because this place would be my home for the next 4 years. I remember my freshman year the first couple of weeks I was so lost, but whenever I looked like I needed help someone would come up to me and ask if I needed help. Whenever (Except for finals week) I walk around everyone seems generally happy and inviting, and school spirit flows throughout the campus. The first 4 months on campus I sat down with new people in the dining halls every time, and it was amazing meeting so many people from diverse backgrounds. My freshman year I lived in one of the high rises with about 30 other guys on my hall, and we became best friends. 2 years later we will still get together and hang out even though we don't live together anymore. My freshman year I would stay up until 2 AM (would not recommend) almost every night playing video games with my hall mates and we would debate different topics while playing. One time we were debating because one of my hall mates would fly around the screen as Kirby while the rest of us fought and used up all our lives. Everyone would pitch in and whenever they spoke it was clear they had well thought out responses and could defend their view points(All of this was while we were still playing). Thirty minutes later I had an idea for a new business so we spent the rest of the night sketching prototypes as well as creating a business plan before we eventually scrapped it around 8 AM and went to sleep. Everyone has been so supportive and there are so many people. You can make a big school small; you can't make a small school big. In a small school once you run out of people you're out of luck, but I will never have that problem at UGA. There are always new people to meet, but whenever I walk around campus I always see people I know, and we stop to chat for a little bit. Also let's say you like something that only 1% of the population is interested in (my case: playing with nerf guns). That means there are 260 other students interested in the same thing you are(we have a humans v. zombies club/ week). Did I mention there are over 700 clubs/organizations on campus? And there are students from almost all backgrounds on campus.

2. Opportunities- I have had opportunities to do things I never imagined while I was in high school. I have had the chance to work on cutting edge research as a freshman. I like to travel so I have traveled to Detroit, Miami, Dallas, Minneapolis, and Silicon Valley for Conferences. Companies will fly me out to visit their campus and interview with them. We also have 3 international campus and over 100 study abroad programs on all 7 continents. I haven't gotten the chance to study abroad yet, but it's on my bucket list. I have had 3 internships all in different areas. I worked at FCA(Fiat-Chrysler) this past summer working with their connected vehicle, and this summer I will be working with Target as a Software Engineer. I also had interviews with other companies so I had my choice of where I wanted to intern this summer.

3. Cost-No matter where you go to school someone is paying for it. You want to make sure that you get the best for your buck. I am paying for college myself and have been able to do so without taking any loans. There are a lot of opportunities for scholarships once you are on campus as well as paid internship and student jobs.

One last thing I want to say is: you can get a great education anywhere you go. You want to look at what differentiates one school from the next(for me it was those 3 factors). I tell people all the time Georgia is the type of place where if you want to stay in your room and say you're going to have a horrible time you can do that. No one is going to stop you; no one will force you to do something you don't want to do, but if you are willing to step outside of your comfort zone and try new things you can have experiences and grow in ways you never imagined were possible.
