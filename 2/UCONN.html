University of Connecticut

编辑



The University of Connecticut (UConn), is a land grant public research university located in Storrs, Connecticut, United States. The University of Connecticut was founded in 1881 and is a Sea Grant university and a member of the Space Grant Consortium. The university serves more than 30,000 students on its six campuses, including more than 8,000 graduate students in multiple programs.
UConn is one of the founding institutions of the Hartford, Connecticut/Springfield, Massachusetts regional economic and cultural partnership alliance known as New England's Knowledge Corridor. UConn is a member of Universitas 21, a global network of 24 research-intensive universities, who work together to foster global citizenship. UConn is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges.
UConn was founded in 1881 as the Storrs Agricultural School, named after two brothers who donated the land for the school. In 1893, the school became a land grant college. In 1939, the name was changed to the University of Connecticut. Over the next decade, social work, nursing, and graduate programs were established, and existing schools of law and pharmacy were absorbed into the university. During the 1960s, UConn Health was established for new medical and dental schools. John Dempsey Hospital opened in Farmington in 1975.
Competing in the American Athletic Conference as the Huskies, UConn has been particularly successful in their Men's and Women's Basketball programs. The Huskies have won a total of 21 NCAA championships.

康涅狄格大学
创建于1881年，前身是斯多斯农学院（Storrs Agricultural School），1939年被正式命名为康涅狄格大学。
现在，康涅狄格大学的主校区仍位于斯托斯（Storrs），占地4104英亩。大学下设13个学院，分别是：农业与自然资源学院、商学院、牙科学院、教育学院、工程学院、美术学院、法学院、人文与科学学院、医学院、护理学院、药学院、农学院、社会工作学院。其中有10个学院位于主校区，法律和社会工作学院位于州首府哈特福德（Hartford），医学院与牙科学院位于法明顿（Farmington），还有5个分校园遍及州内多个城市。大学共设有101个本科专业和80个研究方向。
康涅狄格大学(1903年)　康涅狄格大学是一个注重研究的学校，是卡耐基基金会指定的Ⅰ类研究性公立大学之一，除了院系之外，大学还下设70多个研究中心，2.3万名在校生当中, 研究生近7000人。
康涅狄格大学的教学与研究实力很强，已经连续九年被评为新英格兰地区的最好的大学，每年接到的新生申请比十年前翻了一番。康涅狄格大学向学生提供高质量的课程, 学生的满意度很高。新生一年以后的继续入读率也很高, 2005年入学的新生92都继续在读，这一比例在美国公立大学中排第25。校友毕业以后向学校捐助的比率在全美大学中排第七。

美国大学综合排名，康涅狄格大学排第67名。该校的教育专业具有很高的教学科研水平，教育类排第21位，其中初级教育专业排第18名，特殊教育专业排第21名。此外，热门专业还有社会科学，商业、管理、营销、医疗保健，心理学工程等。
近年来，康涅狄格大学正由州政府注资大力发展学校的硬件设施，兴建了多栋新建筑物和学术设施，并计划建一个以它为中心的大学城，全部计划要到2013年才会完成。
康涅狄格大学创建于 1881年，位于康涅狄格州的斯多斯农学院，1939年正式改名为康涅狄格大学，在Avery Point、Greater Hartford、Stamford、Torrington、Waterbury等地还设有8个分校区。
康涅狄格大学是卡耐基基金会指定的1类研究性公立大学之一。有8个本科学生学位授予点和12个研究生学位授予点，共设有101个本科专业和80个研究方向；此外，该校还有4个职业学位项目。(J.D , M.D , D.M.D, PharmD.)
学校特色
1. 周边地区为学生提供美好的田园风光，临近各大城市和海滩也是学校的地理位置优势。
2. 学校连续六年获得New England地区最佳公立院校称号，并且是在此地区唯一一个提供Environmental Engineering, Computer Engineering和Material Engineering的公立大学。
3. 学校虽然位于遥远的乡村地区，但学生们把自己的创造力发挥到了极致，建立了超过250个社团，Boston和NYC也是他们的最佳选择。学校很重视体育项目，竞技体育成绩优秀，尤其是篮球。
学校概况
康涅狄格大学创建于1881年，前身是斯多斯农学院1939年被正式命名为康涅狄格大学。康涅狄格大学主校区位于康涅狄格州曼斯菲尔德镇的斯托尔斯。新英格兰的科教走廊是哈特福德和斯普林菲尔德附近地区重要的经贸和文化合伙机构，而康涅狄格大学正是该机构的发起者之一。同时，康涅狄格大学也被许多研究高等教育的学者认为是一个“公立版的常青藤名校”。康涅狄格大学是受新英格兰院校协会承认的高等院校，同时也是美国大东部竞技联盟的成员之一。同时为“公立常春藤”大学联盟的一员受到社会高度评价。
地理位置
康涅狄格大学的主校区仍位于斯托斯，占地4104英亩。大学下设13个学院，分别是：农业与自然资源学院、商学院、牙科学院、教育学院、工程学院、美术学院、法学院、人文与科学学院、医学院、护理学院、药学院、农学院、社会工作学院。其中有10个学院位于主校区，法律和社会工作 学院位于州首府哈特福德，医学院与牙科学院位于法明顿。

点评
1. 我非常惊讶的发现康涅狄格大学的计算机科学系居然相当有实力。学校不仅在校园计算机网络构架上投入了大量的财力，而且让每个人知道该怎么合理利用它。经常有来自其他院校计算机科学系的朋友拜访我，发现我们学校在线数据和资源居然如此系统，各个院系和管理部门的数据库居然如此缜密完整，他们都感到非常吃惊。一些非计算机系的朋友也很喜欢在技术中心接受笔记本电脑服务，他们觉得很方便。康涅狄格大学的计算机科学学院是顶尖学院。学院的老师都是理论与应用相结合的教学。
2. 环境以及配套设施最好的是科学系（除物理和数学大楼之外），商学院的大楼也好极了。体育馆很大，你能想到的运动所需器材里面全部都有。餐厅每天提供不同口味的食谱。
3. 康涅狄格大学的大学课程很好。你必须为成功而努力，学校很看重你的成绩。
4. 康涅狄格大学是一所正在不断成长的旗舰大学。体育运动竞赛（以及雇佣的教练）是康涅狄格大学的优势，经常参加全国水平的比赛。这一点使学校成为全美最好和新英格兰最好的学校，但这并不代表它是完美的。这里的天气不怎么样，如果你喜欢秋天，能够适应潮湿的冬天， 那么在这里生活完全没有问题。

Question:

What is it like to attend the University of Connecticut?
Answer:
I really enjoyed my time at UCONN (1997-2001). No, Storrs is not a booming metropolis, but it is relatively pretty, at least topographically. Further, the student body, campus, and educational offerings are all large enough that you don't necessarily need to know where you're heading from the second you get there. You can switch gears socially and academically (I did both) without much difficulty at all. I found the classes to be stimulating enough to hold my interest, but not so demanding as to prevent me from exploring other interests while there. I was also able to obtain a high enough GPA to get scholarship money for law school without expending the type of effort that one might have to at a private school in order to get the same results. Also, going to UCONN was a great opportunity to be around normal people (read: not the blue-bloods, or silver-spooners that you might find at certain nearby urban schools). In short, UCONN is big enough that you can likely find what you want there, if you are willing to look for it. It is challenging enough to hold the interest without consuming one's entire life, or being so competitive as to stifle future opportunity. I wouldn't trade my time there for the world.

I interacted with some of the most interesting people by living on campus and had a really great time working as well as studying. Yes, Basketball is a religion and being a proud husky will creep into your DNA whether you like it or not but that said, its not going to make you a social outcast if you decide to not go to a couple of games. Should I go to Uconn?
Answer:
Yes. UConn was great.

Some non-obvious UConn benefits:

Its in the middle of nowhere
the focus is on your friends and the people around you, not the restaurants, clubs, bars etc...
Pessimists may see this as a bug, but I see it as a feature. I learned how to develop deep friendships, make new friends and how to have fun.
Sports
Having a strong sports tradition on campus was incredibly fun. 85% of the students embraced it.
During March when the basketball team was great, the campus was electric. The same is true for any big game.
The football team also brings a fun atmosphere in the fall. When I was there, they were a little better and new to D1 (new is exciting!!). The tailgates are something to look forward to every week and are some of my most fun memories.
Great school without the obsessive competitiveness of the top schools
I went on to work at some amazing companies that hire people out of elite colleges and then to grad school at MIT. I was always intrigued by the relatively lower stress levels of my fellow UConn alums. See above on learning how to have fun.
To build on this, the top schools often feed schools into some of the most competitive companies. The competition is FIERCE. Again, these are students that have had success their whole life and getting rejected from Goldman FEELS like failure. At UConn I just found working for the best company was not a make it or break it life choice.
That being said, my fellow UConn alums are off doing GREAT THINGS. They just seem a little more balanced than people that went to slightly more competitive schools.
One advantage of going to UConn is actually the fact that you don't get sucked into the jobs the top top schools are hiring at. For example, investment banking is hard to get into at UConn. At Princeton it is relatively easy. Hence a lot of people at Princeton end up trapped in a job they hate working 100 hours a week. At UConn - investment banking is attainable, but the higher bar to break in usually weeds out the people that actually don't want it.

I agree with what Bob Rubino said. It depends on the School - and YOU.

I'm a School of Liberal Arts grad, with a BA is Psychology and minors in Sociology and Art. While getting my BA, I also took as many prerequisites as I could for the School of Pharmacy. (My intention was to get a PhD in psycho-pharmaceutical research - but I ran out of $$ after getting my BA).

UCONN was a great school - but a lot of it was because Liberal Arts allowed me to take some really interesting courses that still applied to my major. My trick to stay engaged with school was to take one "fun" course each semester. By "fun" I mean that I took horseback riding (aka Pleasure Horse Appreciation), two semesters of Russian language, in my very first semester I took a 200s level German Conversation course, lots of Art courses (so many that my portfolio was accepted and I could have majored in Fine Arts), Medical Anthropology, and lots of other interesting stuff. I'm not sure if other schools have this kind of flexibility, or the wide range of courses like UCONN does.

The one drawback about UCONN is that it is HUGE. You will be a very tiny fish in a vast ocean of other students. This however, is what shaped me into the person I am today. No one is going to do things for you, or take you by the hand and guide you. You WILL learn to fend for yourself - or drop out like I saw so many people do.

I came from a small town, and was top of my class in both my small elementary school and small high school. Then - boom - my first class at UCONN was Psych 101. With 400+ other freshmen. There were 400 students in my high school graduating class! The professor started the class by saying how we all thought we were going to be Psych majors, and it was his job to thin us out to about 50 or so by the 200s level. He then pointed to all the doors in the auditorium where the class was being held. Lots of students left at that point, and didn't come back. True story. Imagine - I was 17 years old and knew I was going into Psych since 7th grade. I made it my mission and passion to stick to my plans, and not get cut out by the difficulty of the course material and that professor. And I made it! (and to this day, I still thank him for how he started that first class.)

Interview different professors, and find one that "gets" you. Ask that professor to be your advisor.

In a school that size, you need to become more assertive. You have to to push a lot harder. In the end, you will come out better prepared for life itself, not just a career. Are you willing to do the work?
