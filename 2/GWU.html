George Washington University

编辑



The George Washington University (GW, GWU, or George Washington) is a private, coeducational research university located in the Foggy Bottom neighborhood of Washington, D.C., with two other campuses including the Mount Vernon campus in the Foxhall neighborhood, as well as the Virginia Science & Technology campus in Loudoun County, Virginia. GW is the largest institution of higher education in the District of Columbia.
George Washington, the first president of the United States, had indicated to Congress through various letters, as well as his last will and testament, that he wanted to establish a university within the nation's capital. Washington left fifty shares of the Potowmack Company in his estate for a national university in the District of Columbia. However, due to the company's financial status, the university never received the shares. The university was chartered by an Act of Congress on February 9, 1821, as the Columbian College in the District of Columbia. In 1904, it changed its name to the George Washington University in honor of Washington.
The university has ten colleges and schools: the Columbian College of Arts and Sciences (which includes the Corcoran School of the Arts and Design, the School of Media and Public Affairs, and the Trachtenberg School of Public Policy and Public Administration); the School of Business, the Elliott School of International Affairs, the Milken Institute School of Public Health, the College of Professional Studies (which includes the Graduate School of Political Management), the School of Engineering and Applied Science, the George Washington University School of Medicine & Health Sciences, the Law School, the School of Nursing, and the Graduate School of Education and Human Development.
GW's Graduate School of Political Management is the only school of applied politics in the nation's capital. GW's professional schools and the Elliott School of International Affairs are consistently ranked highly in national and international college rankings lists.
George Washington is consistently ranked by The Princeton Review in the top "Most Politically Active" Schools. Some of the university's graduates have gone on to high positions within both the U.S. government and foreign governments. Notable alumni include Senate Minority Leader Harry Reid, former Secretary of State Colin Powell, and former First Lady Jacqueline Kennedy. The school colors are buff and blue, and the sports teams and current and former students in general are called the Colonials.

乔治华盛顿大学于1821年建校，是美国顶尖的私立综合性大学。其主校区位于美国首都华盛顿特区市中心，毗邻世界银行，国际货币基金组织，国务院，巨白宫只有几个街区。学校现有9个学院，2万多名在读学生，是华盛顿特区内规模最大的大学。
乔治华盛顿大学在华府地区是名声与排名仅次于乔治城大学的一流学府，它培育出的学生在美国乃至全球的政治，外交，法律与商业界具有卓越的贡献。由于其位于美国权利中心-华府市中心的绝佳地理位置，因此大幅提升了该校的政治敏感度，自成立以来，它源源不绝地为白宫，国务院等政府机关以及乔治城大学，世界银行与国际货币基金等重要国际组织注入新血而被誉为“政治家的摇篮”。“世界最贵的贵族学校”，《福布斯》杂志列出的学费最贵的大学名单中，前五名的都是负有盛名的私立大学，第一位就是乔治华盛顿大学，其一年的学费高达6-7万美元，约合40万人民币。这使得乔治华盛顿大学成为当之无愧的“顶级贵族学校”。
作为总统和一名公民，华盛顿相信这个新兴的国家需要一所权威的国立大学，这一直是这位美国第一任总统的心愿，可以使得全国各地的年轻人在这里得到文理科的教育。作为美国的首都，和当时地理上的中部城市，华盛顿认为这座城市是学校的合适所在地。华盛顿留下了50股波托马克公司的股票资助建校。很可惜的是，波托马克公司在股票兑现之前就倒闭了。但是华盛顿的战友们却继承了他的遗志，到处奔走为大学计划游说。他们的努力没有白费，终于说通了当时的总统，和华盛顿同是威廉玛丽学院校友的詹姆斯·门罗和32名国会议员。建校之初，学校坐落在佛罗里达大道上，14和15街之间，距离国会山30分钟路程。当时只有3个教员，1个辅导教师，30个学生。课程包括航海，哲学，英语，拉丁语和希腊语。
1824年，乔治·华盛顿大学举办了首届毕业典礼，美国总统门罗、美国独立战争英雄马贵斯·拉法叶将军亲自到学校捧场。拉法叶是美国独立战争期间协助美军作战的法国将军，他是乔治·华盛顿的好友。很快学校就增设了医学院和法学院。
南北战争的枪声打响后，乔治·华盛顿大学受到重创，招生数量急剧下降，但教授们却不退
缩，在烽火连天的岁月里照样继续坚持上课，甚至经常把学生们请到家里去授课。战事紧张时，乔治·华盛顿大学的校园曾经一度被改建成临时医院，接收过许多北方军的伤兵。当时，有46名毕业生投笔从戎参加了北方军，24名毕业生则选择了敌对的南方军。南北战争把华盛顿从一个小镇变成了一个人口快速增长的城市。
战后，联邦政府雇员急剧增长，同时也促进了学校的增长。学校开设了夜校，增加了新的课程，并且从当地的职员中聘请兼职教员。在学校第六任校长James Clarke Welling（1871-1894）任职期间，学校增设了理学院，开始提供博士学位，并录取了第一名女性学员。1873年，学校举办了招待会庆祝规模扩大，当时的总统尤利塞斯·格兰特（Ulysses S. Grant）到会祝贺。在1893年7月27日，学校财务室安装了第一部电话。
在1904年学校更名为乔治·华盛顿大学，以纪念乔治·华盛顿对该校的创办做出的巨大贡献。
1912年，校址迁往雾谷(Foggy Bottom)---华盛顿的心脏地带位于宾夕法尼亚大道以南的19到24街区，与白宫、世界银行、国际货币基金组织以及众多的联邦机构、国家艺术馆、博物馆等相邻。
Cloyd Heck Marvin （1927-1959）任校长的32年间，学校进行了大规模的基建，增建了Lisner礼堂，一所医院和其他设施。在他任职早期，建设了玫瑰园。并且开始了名为“年度校友赠礼”的项目，此举大大增加了学校的收入。
二十世纪三十年代，乔治·华盛顿大学一度成为世界闻名的物理学家聚集之地，吸引了许多世
界知名物理学家，举办了多个理论物理方面的重要国际会议。爱德华·特勒这位被誉为美国“氢弹之父”的伟大物理学家就在那时与乔治·华盛顿大学结缘。
1935年，为躲避战乱，特勒和妻子米奇举家从欧洲迁往美国，1941年前一直在乔治·华盛顿大学任物理教授。艾略特(Lloyd Hartman Elliott,1965-1988）任校长期间，乔治·华盛顿大学经历了又一个大发展时期。学校捐款从1965年的800万美元增加到1988年的2.24亿美元，新建了3个图书馆，增加了许多研究所，并设立了“大学教授（University Professor）”这一头衔作为对最杰出教授的鼓励。1988年8月1日，斯蒂芬·特拉奇滕伯格(Stephen Joel Trachtenberg,1988-2007）就任学校第15任校长。在他任职期间，学校经历了黄金岁月。由于校长善于筹款，学校的基金从1988年的
2.24亿美元一下子猛增到2004年的7亿美元，已经超过10亿美元。有了雄厚的资金作保证，特拉奇滕伯格新建了许多图书馆、教学楼、医院和学院楼，使它的硬件设施达到世界一流水准坐落于美国的首都，学校接待过众多的国内和国际要人。
2006年－2007年内，在世的历任前总统吉米·卡特、乔治·布什、比尔·克林顿和前任总统乔治·沃克·布什全都到校发表过演讲。此外学校还接待过2008年总统参选人希拉里·克林顿、巴拉克·奥巴马和约翰·爱德华兹

宫殿一样的大学
乔治华盛顿大学在《The Princeton Review》评选出的”宿舍像宫殿一样的学校” 排名“中，名列第10；“学生对社区环境评价高的学校排名” 中，跻身前5。可见乔治华盛顿大学的学生学习生活条件非常优越，可用资源也多得惊人。学校长期不间断投资于更新及翻新校内设施和楼房，使学校长期保持现代化的外观。2013年在校园中建立了退伍军人纪念公园。同时由于校园位于首都心脏地带，学校的安全措施也是十分严密的。全校近200名警察24小时巡逻，校内情况及时通过校内邮件通知全校师生。同时为了学生的安全，校内设有免费校际的士，学生可以随时电话，邮件或者信息订车，也可以直接招手乘坐校内的士，去往各个教学楼，宿舍区，图书馆，体育馆，学生活动中心，商店餐厅，或是行政中心。
学校有六种不同的巴士类型，包括校内的免费巴士以及可以到华盛顿中心地带的付费巴士。学校健身房还提供免费的健身套餐，帮助学生健身
减肥。 学校还有超过400个的小组
俱乐部，包括艺术、学术、宗教、政治等多方面。 学校每天都有几十个为学生的在校活动，像是音乐会、运动会、演讲、辩论和各种社会活动。其中包含了comedy nights——有Jimmy Fallon, Billy Crystal, Jerry Seinfeld, and Robin Williams的演出，著名的歌手比如Girl Talk, Black Eyed Peas, John Mayer, Maroon 5, Sheryl Crow，Train也参与其中。学校还为乔治·华盛顿大学学生提供了相对自由的dining plans,学生可以选择相对适合自己生活方式的套餐
学校还有九个专业图书馆，图书馆藏书量超过200万册，分别为：Burns Law Library伯恩斯法律图书馆，是美国最大的和最有声望的法律学术图书馆之一。Chesapeake Information and Research Library AllianceCIRLA是特拉华州的教育组织和研究机构自发建成的一个非营利性图书馆。Eckles Library at Mount Vernon Campus Gelman Library该图书馆主要服务于在校的学生、教职员工和校友，需要持有有效的图书证才可以进入该图书馆。Himmelfarb Health Sciences Library主要为乔治·华盛顿大学医学中心的学生和教职员工提供信息及服务。Special Collections
University Archives收集、保存那些第一手的、原始的、仅存的资料，包括档案文件、书籍、图像、手抄本、地图、微缩文件、工具书、专题论文、大学出版物、期刊、原始生物资料，以及那些与学院研究没有直接联系的部分信息资料。University Art Galleries该图书馆位于新建的Media and Public Affairs大楼的二层，是一处非常专业的艺术品陈列馆，每年会举行6至8次的展览，包括与院校相关的展览和一些永久性的展览，展览主题往往聚焦华盛顿地区。该展览馆对学院里永久性的一些收藏，例如：重要的油画、雕刻、图表、纺织品、陶制品、传统的家具设备和照片等进行系统管理。Virginia Campus Library Washington Research Library Consortium该图书馆成立于1987年，由华盛顿地区主要的一些大学共同参与，让学生和教职员工能够资源共享，也为参与的大学提供各种服务
美国大学的图书馆是相似的，都有一个大大的空间、方便使用的电脑可供你浏览、写paper甚至发呆，但聊天是比较少见的，大家都很安静。
校友以及学生称呼他们自己为“Colonials”（殖民者)，同时也是学校运动队的吉祥物，GWU的吉祥物打扮像国父乔治-华盛顿一样,这个看起来确是很酷
而河马是学校的象征，也是大学非官方的logo。这源于一个故事，说流经华盛顿的波托马克河曾经盛产河马，据说这种动物一旦出现，就能给庄稼带来好收成，如果能有幸触摸一下它的鼻子，那就会得到好运。乔治·华盛顿和他的夫人经常在自己的农场弗农山庄的池塘边休息，不时也能看到河马从水里跳出来，让夫妻两人高兴不已。当然，故事归故事，传说归传说，美国正式文献从来没有波托马克河盛产河马的记录。但想象力丰富的校长特拉奇滕伯格还是决定让河马成为乔治·华盛顿大学的象征。
同时，乔治华盛顿大学拥有强大的体育队伍，NCAA成员之一，学校的篮球队及橄榄球队一直在美国名列前茅。
平和、低奢，如教堂般的图书馆、如宫殿般的教师，没有诺大的面积甚至有些小巧，但一种独特的韵味确实让人由衷的喜欢，也许是热爱，那就是一种骨子里的高贵和需令人仰慕的内在气质。她的血统是高贵的，是源自于一个伟大的想法，一种旨在对年轻人潜能的激发和对知识的渴求的满足的愿景而存在的，她的存在从一开始就是一个价值的传播和传承.

问题内容:
11月14日，由于谎报数据，乔治·华盛顿大学这个在过去十年一直排在50名左右的美国大学被逐出《美国新闻与世界报道》(US NEWS)排名行列。美国新闻数据调查中心主任罗伯特表示，乔治·华盛顿大学把全国10%强的生源数量提高了20%，该校招生办公室公开宣布78%的新生来自全美顶尖高中，但今年夏天，教务长办公室发现这个数据实际上是58%。
US NEWS负责排名的专家鲍勃·莫斯在博客中指出，基于其提交的虚假数据，乔治·华盛顿大学在今年9月的排名中位列第51位，实际上该校的水平达不到这一位置。另外，乔治·华盛顿大学在过去10多年中一直存在谎报数据行为，因此US NEWS决定将其归为“无排名”一类，这至少将维持到2013年9月。未来乔治·华盛顿大学必须提交准确数据，否则其排名将难以改变。至于如何处置其他高校谎报数据的行为，US NEWS将视情况而定。
实际上，乔治·华盛顿大学的造假并非个案。类似的情况也发生在克莱蒙特·麦肯纳学院。今年，该校校方承认自2006年起便在学生的SAT成绩上造假。最新的排名显示，该校在美国文理学院中排名第9。事件曝光后，编制大学排行榜的Kiplinger杂志将其从2012年的榜单中除名。其他曝出作假丑闻的学校还有于埃默里大学、爱纳学院、贝勒大学、维拉诺瓦大学法学院和伊利诺伊大学。业内人士认为,美国学校数据造假事件层出不穷的元凶是人们对排名的极度重视，引发了大学对排名的疯狂追逐。美国德保罗大学主负责招生的助理副校长博肯斯泰特曾表示：“人们对大学排名的依赖已难以控制。将一所大学的高等教育价值与另一个大学相比较是一件模棱两可的事情，因此家长、学生、咨询顾问都只关注那些他们以为精确，而实际上是假象的东西。”
乔治·华盛顿大学被取消排名引起该校学生的担忧，他们担心这将影响他们未来的就业和继续深造。为此，学生们召开紧急会议，并邀请校长和其他学校高层领导参加。来自英国的运动管理专业大四学生胡戈·谢克特（Hugo Scheckter）表示，在过去近10年中，学校排名一直在50位左右，很多人为了能进入“排名前50”的高校付出了高昂的学费，而如今学校却被取消排名。作为学生，他们想知道究竟发生了什么，谁应该对此次错误负责。 (钟合)

Question:

What are the best reasons to attend the George Washington University?
Answer:
There are several "best reasons" to attend GWU. Here are some of mine:

Wide variety of academic and other programs. It's a major research university located in the heart of Washington, DC offering a tremendous range of courses, degrees and other educational experiences. It attracts world-class faculty in all its departments. I like to describe GWU as a place with a lot of moving parts distributed among three campuses (Foggy Bottom, Mount Vernon and Ashburn, VA) and across several "schools" and "colleges."
Access to all the resources Washington, DC has to offer. As a GWU student you are likely to have full time professors who are well-known experts in their fields and part-time professors who do during the day what they teach at night. My US Presidency class as a GWU undergrad was taught by a man whose "day job" was to direct a small team of non partisan analysts in the Executive Office of the President that advised the President on whether to sign or veto all bills passed by Congress. You can't get much closer to the "real world" of how the Executive Branch functions than that.
Diverse student body. Forget all the complaints about how GWU is "for rich kids." Yes, it has its share of them, but you won't have to look very hard to find students from every State and numerous foreign countries and from a wide range of social/socio-economic backgrounds. You will also not have to look very hard to find other students who share your religious affiliation, if that's important to you.
Active student body. GWU students "do" things. They are politically and otherwise active in many organizations, both on and off campus. There is a certain "energy" among GWU students that I've not seen on other colleges campuses I have visited over the years.
Amazing internship experiences. GWU's location offers many chances to intern with Members of Congress and Senators, with executive branch agencies, and with lobbying/interest groups. Some students at other schools may, if they are lucky, do a "Washington Semester" during their undergraduate studies. As a GWU student you will have "four years of Washington" during which to create an amazing and enviable resume, regardless of your academic major.
While some people think "internships" are only for political science and international affairs majors, math and statistics majors, for example, could intern with technical government agencies such as the Census Bureau, National Center for Health Statistics, National Institute for Standards and Technology, etc. Art majors might intern at the National Gallery of Art, the Smithsonian Institution or another world class facility in the DC area.
Great faculty. Sure, some professors are better in the classroom and friendlier to their students than others. But, I am still in touch with professors I had at GWU 30+ years ago. If you put forth the effort in their classes and take the time to get to know them they will respond in kind.
Cultural and other resources. Where else can you do an internship at the White House in the morning, go to class in the afternoon and then attend a world-class performing arts event at the Kennedy Center, all in one day? DC teems with museums, sports arenas, performing arts centers, libraries, etc. You cannot go to GWU and then complain "there's nothing to do around here at night and on the weekends." Having a Metro (subway) station on campus makes it easy to get to other points in the DC area relatively easily, seven days a week.
"Aware" student body. Many GW students are drawn there by their interest in politics and related pursuits. Some GW students could recite the names of their State's congressional delegation faster than they could the starting roster of a major league sports team.
Improved career services resources for students and alumni. GWU has made important strides in recent years to help both current students and their alumni with their career paths. Funded in large part by generous alumni gifts, these services have been expanded and will continue to expand in the coming years. It will be up to you to take advantage of these tools, but if you do you'll more than likely have a job when you graduate than if you don't. And, those resources will be there for you after you graduate.
Alumni. GWU alumni are found throughout the US and around the world. There are "mentorship" and other programs connecting alumni to each other and to students, as well as an active GWU Alumni Association group on LinkedIn. Alumni relations staff and enthusiastic alumni volunteers arrange social and other events in cities around the world. These sessions make it possible to continue your engagement with GWU no matter where your life path takes you.
Answer:
The faculty is responsive and most importantly, very well integrated in the subject matter they are teaching. Every teacher I had in my public relations courses had worked extensively in the field, so when my work was off on "the wrong track," they didn't say "that's wrong, because page 23 of your textbook said so," they said "possibly, but in my years of experience, this is what happens. What else could happen?"

The courses are specialized and honestly made me feel like I could use the information in the real world. I completed my graduate degree there, and the level of expertise that I had brought back to my company earned me a hefty promotion. It's not just regurgitation, it's real world experience. The classes are well managed, well taught, and well staffed. There's nothing more I could ask for in a college experience.

Question:

Is GWU a school for rich kids?
I am a graduating senior from high school, thinking about to major in International Relations in George Washington University. I come from a lower/middle class family. I have to pay around 4k each year if I go to GWU. I heard GWU has a "rich-kids" reputation. Is this good good for me to attend. I come from a high school with a 60% black, an immigrant background. ....
Answer:
GWU is not a school exclusively "for rich kids." It is "for" (that is, an appropriate place to earn an undergraduate degree) young men and women who want to complete their undergraduate studies at a well-regarded institution of higher learning located in the heart of Washington, DC which offers a wide range of both academic and extracurricular opportunities. Its student body has students from all 50 states and many foreign countries. And, while there are plenty of "rich kids" there, there are also many students from other economic strata.

There are plenty of "rich kids" at virtually every competitive, private university in the United States. You can also find a lot of "rich kids" at non-competitive private universities. And, there are plenty of "rich kids" attending public institutions of higher learning. GWU is no exception.

Just because a college student comes from (however you want to define it) a "rich" family does not make them a bad person. There are many GWU students from well-off families who have been raised to appreciate what they have and to not flaunt their wealth. They want to get as much out of their time at and expense of attending GWU as they can. You can find plenty of rude, arrogant, self-entitled, boorish and unpleasant people all along the socio-economic scale (however you want to define that, too).

Dr. Goldrick-Rab is 100% correct that you need to make sure you fully understand the details of the financial aid package/offer you receive from GWU and from every other school who admits you. If you and your parents have questions about the offer, ask your high school guidance counselor and/or contact the university's admissions/financial aid offices directly. You must clearly understand how much of that package is comprised of student loans you will need to pay back after you graduate.

Another option, if appropriate, would be to reach out to your faith group's minister, priest, imam, rabbi or other representative to the institution's inter-denominational board of chaplains to see if they can help get you the answers you need. (I mention this because when I was GWU's alumni admissions committee chair for Northern CA some years ago I routinely worked, when asked, with Board of Chaplains representatives to make sure incoming students and their families easily found their "faith home" on campus, which greatly aided their transition to college life.) Sometimes a call from a chaplain to one of their contacts in the administration can help burn through the bureaucracy.

Let's face facts: getting an undergraduate education at a competitive research institution located in the heart of a major metropolitan area is going to carry a high price tag. The question often left unanswered (or even unasked) is whether its value is worth its cost. As I understand it, GWU has offered you a generous financial aid package (see comment above about making sure you understand its details). The question for YOU to decide is whether what it offers in terms of academic programs, location, internship/job opportunities, etc. are worth what it will cost you to attend there.

Going to school in the DC area makes a lot of sense if you want to study international relations. You'll have many internship and employment opportunities in that field by being in DC that you won't have by attending a school anywhere else. The political science and international affairs programs at GW (highly interrelated, in places) are among its strongest academic units and very popular with undergraduates. If that's what you want to study you won't go wrong choosing GWU if the finances work out.

I attended GWU as an undergraduate and later earned my master's degree there. Just because some of my classmates came from families with astonishing sums of money (and liked to show it off) did not mean I didn't find friends, activities, internships and paying jobs throughout my time as a student there. You will, too, if you go there. Your grades, your interactions with your professors and your post-undergraduate options need not be governed by the excesses of the small (but highly visible) minority of "rich kids" wherever you choose to go to college.

Go to class, get good grades, and keep your "eye on the prize," which is your diploma at the end of four years. Time wasted being distracted by the antics of your classmates who have seemingly unlimited amounts of money, drink themselves to insensibility several nights of the week, and wear fancy clothes will only detract from the value you can receive from an undergraduate degree, from GWU or any other school.
Answer:
Your situation sounds very similar to mine when I was looking at GWU. I wanted to study international affairs, and I wanted to do it in the middle of DC. There is no.better.place to do that than GWU. Being on campus exposed me to a whole world of privilege and laziness (and entitlement) that I had never known growing up in the middle of Iowa, but that exposure only helped prepare me for the types of things I encounter now in the professional world. I knew plenty of people like me at GW that worked one or two jobs (plus a scholarship) to pay rent and eat, and enjoy the city around us. The Elliott School draws a different crowd than the university at large, and I enjoyed that very much. We became experts at taking advantage of all the wonderful opportunities -- culturally and professionally -- in the city that didn't cost anything (or cost very little). You're not going to get another U.S. city that contributes as much to the educational experience as DC.

Sure, there are some under-motivated privileged kids who live a life you don't understand. But I don't see why that would stop you from taking advantage of what GW and DC have to offer.
