University of Rochester

编辑


The University of Rochester (commonly referred to as U of R or UR) is a private, nonsectarian, research university in Rochester, New York. The university grants undergraduate and graduate degrees, including doctoral and professional degrees. The university has six schools and various interdisciplinary programs.
The University of Rochester is particularly noted for its Eastman School of Music. The university is also home to the Institute of Optics, founded in 1929, the first educational program in the US devoted exclusively to optics. Rochester's Laboratory for Laser Energetics is home to the second most energetic fusion laser in the world.
In its history, five university alumni, two faculty, and one senior research associate at Strong Memorial Hospital have been awarded a Nobel Prize; eight alumni and four faculty members have won a Pulitzer Prize, and 19 faculty members have been awarded a Guggenheim Fellowship. The departments of political science and economics have made a significant and consistent impact on positivist social science since the 1960s; the distinctive, mathematical approach pioneered at Rochester and closely affiliated departments is known as the Rochester school, and Rochester graduates and former affiliates are highly represented at faculties across top economics and political science departments.
The University of Rochester, across all of its schools and campuses, enrolls approximately 5,600 undergraduates and 4,600 graduate students. Its 158 buildings house over 200 academic majors. Additionally, Rochester (along with its affiliated Strong Health System) is the largest employer in the Greater Rochester area and the sixth largest employer in New York.

本人就读于罗村大学周围某一私立高中，并在14年秋天在罗村大学选了门数学课。 也就是说我有很长的时间呆在UR里面，算是半个罗大的人吧。
作为半个罗大的学生，我的判断可能很主观。
1.学生学习能力
在罗大选了门概率论。众所周知，概率论是门实用性智障课程。也就是说，对于任何Calc和Combinatorics基础好一点的人，这门课考试不会低于95， 成绩不会低于A。班上大多数人是大二学生，都是学过Calc2和Combinatorics。教授也相当给力，逻辑清晰，板书工整。但是第一个期中考试结果出来的时候我就已经吓尿了(真的吓尿了）----仅仅是考排列组合和容斥原理平均分就只有75。但是亚裔基本不是100就是90.
第二个期中考试和期末考试的平均分就更吓人了:只有60+。这还是班上有20%的中国人的情况下的平均分。后来我又去听了下Stochastic Process和一些CS课。基本也是一模一样的情况。
相对而言，我在CMU与同是大二的同学同学Diff Eq的时候就感觉到竞争压力相对大一点，平均分相对高很多。 虽说我上面做的这个比较相当不好，因为CMU是以理工闻名而UR的厉害之处在别的地方。
但是我还是对UR学生(非亚裔)的逻辑能力产生了极大的怀疑！如果连作为最常用的逻辑模型数学都学不好的话，那什么逻辑能学好？
2.校园环境
来罗村三年，年年下雪。秋天还风和日丽，随处草坪上都能看到美女躺在草地上晒太阳。但是一到冬天，真的是一片死寂。校园的阴郁成都不比Cornell好多少。
3. 实习机会
据我所知，UR的实习机会和research opportunity相当多。 UR毕竟是罗村最大的“企业”，基本罗村企业的人才都是有UR输送。
4.本科生录取
因为本人自己也在申请大学，而且随着国内要留美读书的人越来越多，所以也来谈谈这个。 罗村大学在上个世纪的排名相当靠前，但是毕竟随着柯达和施乐的没落，罗切斯特大学的吸引力也逐渐减少。我有两个同学今年Early Decision被UR录取了，过程感觉相对而言轻松。 作为一个排名30的大学，UR让我感觉其录取标准并没有那么高。感觉只要有可靠证明，学术和综合能力强，即使sat1800，act 25 都能进这学校。
但是据说好的program还是highly selective，难度也是不容小觑。
大势所趋之下，出国留学的基本分为两类：商与理工。作为准备学数学理工的屌丝虽说还是申请了这所大学，不过只准备把其当做备胎。美国数理比其好的学校多了去了，什么MIT，Stanford,Cal Tech(暂时还高攀不起)，HMC，CMU，GT(本屌丝现在比较实际的选择）什么的绝对能塑造出一个更好的科学家或者工程师。学商的就不用谈了，排名什么的很能说明问题。
但是要申请UR的理由也很充分。医科和光学很优秀，而且同时大多数热门专业都处于美国前50甚至前30水平。所以就业以及之后的深造相对而言比那些没有底蕴的Rank30+还是要好多了。

Question:

What are the best reasons to attend the University of Rochester?
Answer:
It's:

1. A research university
2. with a felxible curriculum
3. that doesn't charge per credit hour.


There are two kinds of students that really thrive here. The first kind are students who find a specific interest, and take advantage of the research opportunities. (You'll meet more people who work part time in a lab than work part time in the book store.) The second kind are students who have multiple interests (sometimes related, sometimes not) and explore them all in depth. I graduated in four years (plus one summer) with three majors, a minor, and a phd-level formal modeling sequence under my belt. That's not something I could have done at a school that forced me to take literature classes.

Disclaimer: Although the U of R is a research university, it's a relatively small one, so if there is a particular field you are interested in visit the department and talk to some professors. Make sure there are people working on/offering classes in things that interest you. That's much more important than the campus tour.

Good luck, whereever you end up going. If you're interested in computer science or some sort of quantitative social science, feel free to privately message me

PS: It also helps if you like snow.
Answer:
Attend UR if you want to participate in scientific research (and maybe even its commercialization) as an undergrad.

The most interesting thing about UR is how much research is happening relative to the small size of the undergraduate college. The last time I looked, UR had about as many grad students (a proxy for amount of research) as undergrads. Compare this to other schools (e.g., Cornell, which has many times more undergrads than grad students), and you'll see there is far more research occuring per undergrad at UR than just about any other college in the country.

This dynamic makes it fairly simple to get a paying job in a lab as an undergrad. That's right - make $10 / hr (2003 rates) to buoy your college lifestyle while building a professionally relevant resume during the school year.

At UR, I worked in life science labs and human subjects research for 2 years (frosh - soph!), which was enough to suggest this life was not for me (a research career requires saintly amounts of patience that I do not possess). But I quickly got an internship in the Tech Transfer Office (which is top 10 in the country in terms of licensing revenue) and I loved it! It turned into an entire career working at the intersection of science and business that has been very rewarding. And I think I owe that to UR.
