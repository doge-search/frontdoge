University of Massachusetts Amherst

编辑



The University of Massachusetts Amherst (otherwise known as UMass Amherst or simply UMass) is a public research and land-grant university in Amherst, Massachusetts, United States, and the flagship of the University of Massachusetts system. With approximately 1,300 faculty members and more than 29,000 students, UMass Amherst is the largest public university in New England and is ranked the 29th best public university in the nation.
The university offers bachelor's degrees, master's degrees, and doctoral degrees in 111 undergraduate, 75 master's and 47 doctoral programs in nine schools and colleges. The main campus is situated north of downtown Amherst. In a 2009 article for MSN.com, Amherst was ranked first in Best College Towns in the United States. In 2012, U.S. News and World Report ranked Amherst among the Top 10 Great College Towns in America. It is also a member of the Five College Consortium.
The University of Massachusetts Amherst is categorized as a Research University with Very High research activity by the Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching. In fiscal year 2014, UMass Amherst had research expenditures exceeding $200 million.
UMass Amherst sports teams are called the Minutemen and Minutewomen, the colors being maroon, black, and white; the school mascot is Sam the Minuteman. All teams participate in NCAA Division I. The university is a member of the Atlantic 10 Conference, while playing ice hockey in Hockey East and football in the FBS level Mid-American Conference (MAC).

在这里读书两年，印象最深的三样: 枫叶、暴风雪，还有大龙虾。
首先说枫叶，学校的地理位置在麻省西边的先锋谷 (Pioneer Valley) 的安默斯特镇 (Amherst) 大约十分钟左右车程外，新英格兰地区那几个州本来就是美国有名的赏枫胜地，加上学校周围的山林多，九十月份的时候，真的是景色一流。(另外，说说这个安默斯特镇，是一个典型的东海岸小镇，白人居多，那个出了个总统外加各界名流的美国著名文理学院 Amherst College 也坐落在这里，值得一提的是，它和UMass Amherst以及另外三个文理学院共同组成了一个五校联盟，各校学生之间都可以相互选课。)

Question:

Do I have a shot at UMass Amherst's CS program for the MS/PhD program with a slightly above average GPA (7.3/10)?
Please read question details
I've recently finished my B.Tech in CS from a top-25 school in India.
I had applied for my MS for Fall 15 but was rejected from all the colleges I had applied to for MS in CS (ML specialization), including UMass, Amherst.

I had decent GRE and TOEFL scores (321, 108) and few exploratory projects in ML. They were only elementary as I did not have any formal courses in ML and whatever I've learnt is by self-tutelage.
I believe my SOP was strong but 2 of my LORs were from Profs from my college who are unknown.

I suspect my mediocre GPA. (6.9/10) when I had applied for Fall 15.

Since then, my GPA has improved to a 7.3, have interned at IISc Bangalore, IIIT-H and am currently interning at Xerox Research Centre India (XRCI).

I haven't been able to publish from my internships yet, but I'm hoping I can change that around here at XRCI.

Do I have a fair shot at being admitted to UMass's MS only or MS/PhD track?
Should I bother applying again?
Answer:
UMass Amerst's CS program is well renowned for Artificial Intelligence. If you are considering to perform research in AI and Machine learning, you should definitely apply to their program. It's quite competitive and only about 10% from the applicant pool gets admitted. Also, the applicant is admitted based on the overall application package that you provide.

For you, your GPA depends on the university from which you have passed out. A 7.3 may be considered as an average GPA for students passing out from your university, whereas it maybe considered exceptional for students passing out from a different university with the same GPA. So no one can really say as to why you got rejected.

However, if you have completed your internship with some quality research work to show, it would definitely help if you plan on applying once again. Make your Statement of Purpose to the point and get strong recommendation letters from your professors. Your GRE and TOEFL scores are decent as well, which helps in making you a competitive applicant.
Answer:
You may get admitted to MS only track, MS only is not that hard, but for MS/PhD it is very tough. UMass is among the top 20 or top 15 programs when it comes to AI (I got in, with 6.27 gps, 3.9/4.0 masters gpa, 8 international publications --- one in topmost journal in robotics) --- see for more details --- Sanjeev Sharma's answer to What are my chances to get into University of California, Irvine and University of Massachusetts Amherst for an MS in CS (machine learning or AI) with the profile mentioned below?

P.S. With that GPA, it would be nearly impossible to get in without a publication. Even if you had say 9.5 GPA or something, it would have been very very hard to get in --- it is among top 15 programs --- very hard without a publication.
