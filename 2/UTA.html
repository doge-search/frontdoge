University of Texas Arlington

编辑


The University of Texas at Arlington (UT Arlington or UTA) is a public research university located in Arlington, Texas. The campus is in the Dallas-Fort Worth-Arlington metropolitan area, and is adjacent to downtown Arlington. The university was founded in 1895 and was in the Texas A&M University System for several decades until joining The University of Texas System in 1965.
In Fall 2015, the campus student population of 37,008 was the second-largest in the UT System. The Carnegie Foundation in 2016 classified UT Arlington in the category of "R-1: Doctoral Universities - Highest Research Activity." Only 115 institutions in the nation are listed in that category which is often referred to as "tier one." The Chronicle of Higher Education named the university one of the fastest growing public research universities in the nation. UTA offers 81 baccalaureate, 71 masters, and 31 doctoral degrees.
The university operates the Fort Worth Education Center in downtown Fort Worth and the UTA Research Institute in River Bend Park in eastern Fort Worth.

Question:

How good is the MS in CS at the University of Texas at Arlington?
in terms of -Ranking -RA/TA Funding -Cost of living + Fees -Co-Ops & Job Opportunities. I’m going to apply for Fall 2015 and I’m confused about it. Can you provide your valuable suggestion?
Answer:
My background: I am a CS Graduate student at UTA, started 2014 Fall, have 2.5 years of work experience, not good undergrad GPA and GRE 304 (Q-163, V-141, Essay-2.5)

In terms of ranking on US News website, UTA is ranked at 90th position in US (University of Texas--Arlington), for research work in Data Mining 20th (Page on microsoft.com) and for research work in machine learning & pattern recognition 22nd (Page on microsoft.com). Read about these rankings from Computer Science and Engineering ranking section.

Getting RA/TA position is very difficult unless you are interested in doing Thesis or working on a project under a professor.

Cost of living is relatively cheaper when compared to other parts of US. one bed room apartment costs 650-800$ and two bed room apartment costs around 950-1200$.

Tuition Fees is approximately 9000$ per semester for 9 credits (including student insurance), which is again cheaper than other US universities.

Currently, I am applying for internships/Co-Op positions, but you are on your own for the application process. Assuming you are an international student, here in US there won't be campus placements like in India. Few companies come to the Job Fair on a particular day (all on one day), you have to submit your resume, they will ask you one or two questions, if they like you they will continue the interview process offline later. But, nothing more than that! No job offers will be made on the same day.

Regarding companies coming to UTA job fair, there are not many companies that come to UTA. Few who came last Fall are Microsoft, HP & indeed (I don't remember the whole list). You are on your own, you have to apply to other companies on their portal to get an internship/job.

Don't get bogged down, but I won first place in Microsoft college coding competition at UTA. They conducted it the day after the Job Fair, I haven't even got an interview call from them.

I know a few who graduated at UTA and are working at Yahoo, American Airlines, Goldman Sachs etc. So, I guess one can find a job on their own. FYI, many companies like Google, Amazon visit better ranked universities like USC, OSU, so getting an interview is more easier there.

Regarding you applying to UTA, if you have a better option, you go ahead with that or else if you think you can improve your profile, do it and apply for next term. My undergrad scores weren't good and I thought improving my GRE Verbal score will be difficult for me, so, I accepted my admission.

All the best! If you need any other information comment on this.
