Case Western Reserve University

编辑



Case Western Reserve University (also known as Case Western Reserve, Case Western, Case, and CWRU) is a private doctorate-granting university in Cleveland, Ohio. The university was created in 1967 by the federation of Case Institute of Technology (founded in 1881 by Leonard Case Jr.) and Western Reserve University (founded in 1826 in the area that was once the Connecticut Western Reserve). TIME magazine described the merger as the creation of "Cleveland's Big-Leaguer" university.
In U.S. News & World Report's 2015 rankings, Case Western Reserve's undergraduate program ranked 37th among national universities. The University is associated with 16 Nobel laureates. Other notable alumni include Paul Buchheit, creator and lead developer of Gmail, founder of FriendFeed, and Partner at YCombinator; Craig Newmark, founder of craigslist.org; Pete Koomen, the co-founder and CTO of Optimizely; and Peter Tippett, who developed the anti-virus software Vaccine, which Symantec purchased and turned into the popular Norton AntiVirus. Case Western Reserve is particularly well known for its medical school, business school, dental school, law school, Frances Payne Bolton School of Nursing (named for former U.S. Representative Frances P. Bolton), Department of Biomedical Engineering and its biomedical teaching and research capabilities. It is also a leading institution for research in electrochemistry and electrochemical engineering. Case Western Reserve is a member of the Association of American Universities.
The university is approximately five miles (8 km) east of downtown Cleveland in the neighborhood known as University Circle, a 550-acre (220 ha) area containing numerous other educational, medical, and cultural institutions. Case Western Reserve has a number of programs taught in conjunction with University Circle institutions, including the Cleveland Clinic, the University Hospitals of Cleveland, the Louis Stokes Cleveland Department of Veteran's Affairs Medical Center, Cleveland Institute of Music, the Cleveland Hearing & Speech Center, the Cleveland Museum of Art, the Cleveland Institute of Art, the Cleveland Museum of Natural History, and the Cleveland Play House. Severance Hall, home of the Cleveland Orchestra, is also on the Case Western Reserve University campus.
The famous Michelson-Morley interferometer experiment was conducted in 1887 in the basement of a campus dormitory by Albert A. Michelson of Case School of Applied Science and Edward W. Morley of Western Reserve University. This experiment proved the non-existence of the luminiferous ether and was later understood as convincing evidence in support of special relativity as proposed by Albert Einstein in 1905. Michelson became the first American to win a Nobel Prize in science. The commemorative Michelson-Morley Memorial Fountain as well as an Ohio Historical Marker are located on campus, near where the actual experiment was performed.

Case Western Reserve凯斯西储本身就是一个不平衡的学校，即使在美国也是毁誉参半。为了这个疑问，我是去过Case实地访问，和一些人聊过天求证了一下。以下纯属个人观点。
学校在克利夫兰，这个地方受美国经济影响还是很明显的。虽然克利夫兰在俄亥俄州也算大城市，但和洛杉矶、纽约等一线国际化大城市不能比，和亚利桑那、科罗拉多和德克萨斯之类的有大量新型技术产业支持的城市也不能比。具体来说，大克利夫兰城市地区的失业率是7.2%，明显高于全美城市的6.8%平均失业率，在美国劳工部划定的372个有统计失业率的城市区划中排名第252名，状况很不理想，位居中下游。因此，可以看出克利夫兰地区的经济形势和城市景气度堪忧。与此相关的大学和高等教育也必定受地域、城市和资源所限制，导致这个学校在美国和中国人心目中的影响力都不足。
学校是私立的中小型大学，虽然是综合性大学，但本科生人数算是偏少的。整个学校1万人出头，本科生就4000多。所以她符合一切“麻雀虽小、五脏俱全”的私立学校的描述，良好的师生比例、强悍的软硬件、美丽的校园之类的。既然如此，为什么中国和美国学生都有人不很喜欢这个学校呢？
坦白说，除了地域问题以外，个人认为是中国留美本科生的功利心和对就业、绿卡等的急切需求使得我们不太愿意选择这个学校，特别是自视甚高的精英群体，很难放下一些对名利和物质的追求来放平心态去看这所大学并在其中生存。凯斯西储这所大学很明显是一所研究型大学，她最强的科目如大家所说，莫过于物理、生物、化学、生物化学和医学等所谓的physical science或者叫natural sciences，而且在大量的理论科学和纯学术的科研项目中，她拥有着悠久的历史和深远的学术影响。比如说凯斯西储先后出过17位诺贝尔奖得主，分别是生理/医学、物理、化学和经济学等学科。如果学生矢志于钻研理科和医学，埋头于学术，那么静心于凯斯西储校园并攻读博士甚至博士后，作出比较好的科研成绩并最终进入研究所或专业领域，是非常好的选择。
所以，如果很需要就业的资源，很注重将来毕业就找到高薪工作或者去银行、四大、500强的这种想法，凯斯西储满足不了，一来克利夫兰本来就不景气，美国人自己还找不着工作呢，二来学校本来就不是这个风格，何来满足呢？该校起码35-40%的人都在读生物、生化或者pre-med一类的东西，都是长期抗战，拿奖学金读博士读医学院的打算。学校根本也没怎么希望说大力去想办法照顾这些一毕业就要去找工作拿绿卡的国际学生，说实话也做不到。这个H-1签证和工作的问题，是全美国留学生的老大难问题了。

大学简介
凯斯西储大学(Case Western Reserve University)位于俄亥俄州的克里夫兰，是美国一所顶级私立研究型大学、一级国家级大学，位于俄亥俄州的克里夫兰，在北美洲有相当的名望。该校成立于1967年，是凯斯理工学院及西储大学两校合并而成，并历来以极度严谨的学风，极度严苛的课程和杰出闻名的研究闻名，是俄亥俄州名副其实的第一学府。该校诺贝尔得主有16位之多，美国第一位本土诺贝尔奖获得者也出自该校(1907年诺贝尔物理学奖)。在美国所有大学里历来综合排名40名左右，其开设的法律类、管理类、医学类、护理类专业享有很高的业界认可度。
凯斯西储大学里充斥着实习、就业、体验多元文化的机会。在过去的3年里，平均就业率、研究生院和专业院校录取率是90%。学校距离克里夫兰市中心仅仅5英里， 这里是涉足医疗、生物技术科学的绝佳之地。在克里夫兰，学生有机会进入美国伊顿公司(Eaton Corporation)、密钥银行(KeyBank)、美铝公司(Alcoa)、以及大量私企工作。该校的毕业生还有的进入哈佛、斯坦福、耶鲁、麻省理工、加州理工学院等知名高等学府继续深造。

Question:

How good is the Computer Science program at Case Western Reserve University? Is job placement good after your undergrad? Would it be viable to double major in Physics?
Answer:
The Comp Sci department is pretty strong, and the good thing about the major at CWRU is that it leaves you a lot of spaces for electives, which could easily be used for a double major. In fact, I'd say that CS is the easiest major to double with, just because of the amount of free space in your schedule. I don't know much about the physics majors (as you can imagine, it's not the largest department).

According to a survey of the class of 2014, 7% of CS students went on to grad school and 87% had an industry job lined up before graduation. 0% were listed as "available for employment", so all those who wanted jobs got them. The national job placement average was below 70% for CS majors in the same year, according to Forbes. I'm not a stats major, but that seems pretty good to me.

I personally love being a CS major at CWRU. The professors are great, and your can get an internship or research position your freshman year. I'm a rising sophomore, and I already had an internship lined up around midterms my second semester. There are a lot of opportunities here.
