Kansas State University

编辑



Kansas State University, commonly shortened to Kansas State or K-State, is a public doctoral university with its main campus in Manhattan, Kansas, United States. Kansas State was opened as the state's land-grant college in 1863 the first public institution of higher learning in the state of Kansas. It had a record high enrollment of 24,766 students for the Fall 2014 semester.
Branch campuses are in Salina and Olathe. The Kansas State University Polytechnic Campus in Salina is home to the College of Technology and Aviation. The Olathe Innovation Campus is the academic research presence within the Kansas Bioscience Park, where graduate students participate in research bioenergy, animal health, plant science and food safety and security.
The university is classified as a research university with highest research activity (R1) by the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education. Kansas State's academic offerings are administered through nine colleges, including the College of Veterinary Medicine and the College of Technology and Aviation in Salina. Graduate degrees offered include 65 master's degree programs and 45 doctoral degrees.

Question:

What are the best reasons to attend Kansas State?
Answer:
Research, Tradition, and Culture

I'll discuss each in succession:

Research: In some departments at K-state, the research being done is world-class. Not just "good for a state school in the Midwest(this is true of many)" but actually groundbreaking time and time-again. One such is the plant-pathology department. K-state is one of the absolute best places to do this sort of research. There are other very specific research areas like this.

Tradition: As hard as it is for me to understand, K-state has a beautiful tradition. Their alumni engagement ranks in the highest amongst state schools, their integration with their sport tradition is ubiquitous, and their value tradition is so consistent it can feel annoyingly archaic. Some people really value this, and it's no wonder why attending K-state feels like joining a family, more-so than many other universities that I've interacted with in various ways.

Culture: Say what you will about the Midwest, but their reputation for friendliness and joviality is not unwarranted. I am not claiming the culture is without fault--in fact, the culture was not a good fit for me personally--but for many, many people, this culture is extremely up-lifting. Especially for those who were raised in the Midwest, K-state's culture is really a shining star among college experiences in the area. Many of the other schools of K-states approximate size in the area have considerably worse reputations for student culture--and national reputations reflect that.

It's hard to predict what is important to you, but some of these might be. One more detail to be considered: K-state is inexpensive!

I attended Kansas State University during 2009-2011. I completed my MS Industrial and Manufacturing Systems Engineering. Here are the reasons I feel one should attend Kansas State for.


1. Tuition Fees

The main reason I chose K-State was because of the tuition fees being lesser as compared to other schools of the same ranking. I had admits from Texas Tech, Wayne State but the fees at K-State were much lesser.

2. Living Costs

Manhattan is a college town in the state of Kansas. Most of the population is student population. The living costs are decent for students. On an average the monthly living costs do not extend beyond 600 to 650 dollars. Manhattan is also ranked among one of the best college towns in America.



3. Research

Kansas State University is one of the top universities in state of Kansas. Being a state university, lot of state research projects are awarded to different departments of the university. Civil Engineering Department of Kansas State is known nationwide for their research in Transportation Engineering along with other good schools like Texas A&M. Other departments of engineering also conduct extensive research on various areas.

Kansas State University was established as an agricultural university in 1863. Not to mention, there has been extensive research in Agricultural Engineering at K-State.

The university is also home to National Bio and Agro-Defense Facility (NBAF).

4. Variety of Courses

K-State offers more than 250 undergraduate programs and about 60 masters degree programs.

5. Campus

K-state has one of the beautiful campuses in the country. K-State campus was ranked 18th by Business Insider under their 20 Best College Campuses in US. The Hale Library of K-State is also one of the amazing college libraries.


6. Football

If you are a football lover, you will love your time at K-State. K-State has a very good football program under the guidance of Coach Bill Snyder. NFL Players, Jordy Nelson, Tyler Locket have played college football at K-State.
