University of Central Florida

编辑



The University of Central Florida (UCF) is an American public research university in Orlando, Florida. It is the largest university in the United States by undergraduate enrollment, and the second largest by total enrollment.
Founded in 1963, UCF opened to provide personnel to support the U.S. space program at the Kennedy Space Center and Cape Canaveral Air Force Station on Florida's Space Coast. As its academic scope broadened, it was renamed from Florida Technological University to the University of Central Florida in 1978. While initial enrollment was only 1,948 students, enrollment today amounts to some 60,821 students from 140 countries and all 50 states and Washington, D.C. The majority of the student population is located on the university's main campus just 13 miles (21 km) east-northeast of downtown Orlando, and 55 miles (89 km) southwest of Daytona Beach.
The university offers over 200 degrees through thirteen colleges and twelve satellite campuses in Central Florida. Since its founding, UCF has awarded almost 280,000 degrees, including 50,000 graduate and professional degrees, to over 240,000 alumni worldwide.
UCF is a space-grant university, and has made significant research contributions to engineering, optics, simulation, digital media, business administration, education, hospitality management, and the arts. Its official colors are black and gold, and the university logo is a Pegasus, which "symbolizes the university's vision of limitless possibilities." The university's intercollegiate sports teams, commonly known as the "UCF Knights" and represented by mascot Knightro, compete in National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) Division I and the American Athletic Conference.
As of 2016, UCF is ranked 91st among all public universities by U.S. News & World Report.

以下为简要内容：
问：大四还是已经毕业
我说final year。
问：现在开始做毕设没
我说在写research proposal，可能还是关于security的。

总结：
因为之前问了，说是informal chat...我也不是很想做这个方向，所以还算轻松。
就是问到phd plan和conquer difficulty的时候有点慌了，表达的不清楚。

有几点体会：
1. 如果不是一个研究方向，电面相对容易。
首先，介绍自己的research比较轻松，老师不可能问深入的问题；
其次，老师的目的是问有没有兴趣(潜台词：愿不愿意给他干活)，其他方面的考察自然会水一些；
第三，既然是反套，老师要么缺人要么你的background不错，只要不紧张就没大的问题。

2.准备很重要！
这一点大家都说到了。我觉得，经典问题的回答可以提升老师对你的整体印象，一个有逻辑的清楚的表达会让听者感到轻松和愉悦。而且，这部分完全可以在短时间内提升，没必要扔了。所以还是那句话，前期准备必不可少！

3.一回生二回熟。
谁能不紧张？最好的办法就是多面几次，越往后越轻松。找过工作的肯定都了解，面霸之所以成为面霸，靠的就是次数。第一次紧张了，第二次好多了，第三次不怕了，第四次就无所谓了...

4. 试着掌握谈话的主动权。
我觉得，和老师的面试都可以如此。虽然地位不可能平等，但毕竟也是双向选择。我们没有理由显得很被动(虽然就是很被动)。掌握了主动权，首先会不紧张了，可以把话题转到自己会说的东西上，而且还有interaction比较像聊电话。我有个小方法，一是总结老师的话换个说法说出来，看他接着问什么说什么。另一种是根据他的话再问他问题。这在电话交流中既简单又有效。


完~

Question:

What engineering school is better, UCF or RIT?
Answer:
UCF is an excellent university.

I'm currently a freshman majoring in Computer Science there. Sure, I'm not an engineering major per se, but I do share the same college (i.e. the College of Engineering and Computer Science).

First and foremost, none of my classes have exceeded 700 students. In fact, not even my general education classes have even come close to that size. Don't get me wrong, classes have the potential to get "large" -- but not that large. The largest class I've seen contained just under 300 students. You might want to cross-check that with those source of yours, Robert Allen.

Needless to say, if you want a more intimate learning environment RIT may be a better fit. However, if you meet the criterium, the Burnett's Honors College should give you the small college experience that you are looking for. (The Burnett Honors College - TBHC)

Location is also an important factor to consider when selecting a university. I can't speak about RIT, but being in Orlando has its benefits. The numerous companies located both on and adjacent to the campus are invaluable assets. Engineering firms such as Lockheed Martin, NASA, and Boeing frequent the campus looking for new recruits. If you're ambitious, there is even potential to land an internship within your field right away.

At the end of the day, "better" is subjective. Admittedly, UCF wasn't my first choice. To be honest, it was my last choice. For the longest time I was depressed that none of the other schools I applied to (e.g. Georgia Tech, USC, etc) accepted me.

These days, I couldn't be happier. I've had opportunities here that I would have never expected to have. For me, it isn't just about the classes. It's about the opportunities, and UCF stands for opportunity.
Answer:
RIT is an esteemed university.
UCF is generally regarded as a degree mill.

RIT class sizes hover around 30.
UCF class sizes regularly exceed 700.

If you want a cheap degree, go to UCF.

If you want a quality education and a degree with brand recognition, go to RIT.

Source: I have a number of friends from both schools with whom I've discussed the merits


Question:

How is the graduate program in computer science at University of Central Florida in terms of funding and getting in touch with many tech companies for internships/jobs?
Answer:
Being a current graduate CS student, I would say that the program is decent. The coursework looks good on paper but when you come here, you will only find a handful of those subjects being offered. That being said, some of the forensic and theoretical computer science as well as Artificial Intelligence courses are pretty good.

Now coming to the actual question, UCF CS department enjoys a lot of funding and professors are generous enough to fund students. While being a master's student it is difficult to get full tuition waiver, I have not seen any PhD candidate without a tuition waiver and reasonable stipend(Keep in mind that expenses in Orlando are not that high)

I myself being a Masters student, found an OPS research assistant job which offers me a reasonable stipend and opportunity to work on projects. If you choose MS with Thesis option, you might get tuition waiver as well.

Regarding jobs and internships, UCF has its own reputation in the industry mainly because of undergrads and clubs like ACM, Programming Team and Hack UCF. Top companies like Google, Microsoft often visit campus where you get a chance to network and socialize with recruiters. But it really depends on your profile if you are aiming a TOP company. I have seen people getting internships at Disney during career fair because their skill sets aligned really well with the requirements. However, you should always keep in mind that Orlando is certainly not "The Valley". And hence there are a limited number of local jobs if that's what you are aiming for.

I hope that the answer sheds lights on pros and cons of graduate program at UCF in general.
