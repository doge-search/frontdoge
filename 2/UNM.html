The University of New Mexico (also referred to as UNM) is a public research university located in Albuquerque, New Mexico. It is New Mexico's flagship research institution, the largest post-secondary institution in the state in total enrollment across all campuses as of 2012, and one of the state's largest employers.
Founded in 1889, UNM offers bachelor's, master's, doctoral, and professional degree programs in a wide variety of fields. Its Albuquerque campus currently encompasses over 600 acres (2.4 km2), and there are branch campuses in Gallup, Los Alamos, Rio Rancho, Taos, and Los Lunas.Coordinates: 35.08389 106.61861 UNM is categorized as an RU/VH Research University (very high research activity) in the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education, with three Nobel laureates affiliated or associated.

Question:

Is University of New Mexico a good school?
Answer:
Yes, it is a good school. But all colleges and universities are good schools. You don't say what field you are interested in or what your overall education plans are. You can research the top schools for your field and find a school that way.

The UNM main campus is in the heart of Albuquerque. Twenty-Five years ago when I attended the school it was just on the edge of som pretty dodgy neighborhoods. With all the development since then, it is a thriving well to do community.

Back to school itself. You can go to a small school that has no reputation at all and still get an excellent education. While quality teaches and a known program help, the qualify of your education relies on you. If you don't take an active role in your education then it doesn't matter what the school's reputation is.

If you don't understand something speak up. If the material is too simple, then do your own research at the library or ask the instructor more in depth study pointers/materials.
Answer:
Yes, it is a good college.

UNM is the flagship public university in New Mexico.

It is located in Albuquerque and is close to a very large US Air Force engineering and scientific research center at Kirtland AFB and to a very large US Department of Energy Lab at Sandia (co-located at Kirtland AFB) and not too far from Las Alamos National Lab and the White Sands Missile Range. Therefore, there are plenty of opportunities for funded research.
