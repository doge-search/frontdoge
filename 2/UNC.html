The University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, also known as UNC, or simply Carolina, is a public research university located in Chapel Hill, North Carolina, United States. After being chartered in 1789, the university first began enrolling students in 1795, which also allows it to be one of three schools to claim the title of the oldest public university in the United States. North Carolina is one of the original eight Public Ivy schools that are claimed to provide an Ivy League experience for a public schooling price.
The first public institution of higher education in North Carolina, the school opened its doors to students on February 12, 1795. The university offers degrees in over 70 courses of study through fourteen colleges and the College of Arts and Sciences. All undergraduates receive a liberal arts education and have the option to pursue a major within the professional schools of the university or within the College of Arts and Sciences from the time they obtain junior status. Under the leadership of President Kemp Plummer Battle, in 1877 North Carolina became coeducational and began the process of desegregation in 1951 when African-American graduate students were admitted under Chancellor Robert Burton House. In 1952, North Carolina opened its own hospital, UNC Health Care, for research and treatment, and has since specialized in cancer care. The school's students, alumni, and sports teams are known as "Tar Heels".
The campus of North Carolina is located in Chapel Hill, North Carolina, a university town. The campus covers 729 acres (3 km2) over Chapel Hill's downtown area, encompassing places like the Morehead Planetarium and the many stores and shops located on Franklin Street. Students can participate in over 550 officially recognized student organizations. The student-run newspaper The Daily Tar Heel has won national awards for collegiate media, while the student radio station WXYC provided the world's first internet radio broadcast. North Carolina is one of the charter members of the Atlantic Coast Conference, which was founded on June 14, 1953. Competing athletically as the Tar Heels, North Carolina has achieved great success in sports, most notably in men's basketball, women's soccer, and women's field hockey.

下面我将从北卡教堂山的学习和衣食住行等方面来描述一下我的感受：
学习
也许和高中不一样的是，在大学里你会见到很多眼界、追求、专业方向与你不同的人。如果你来自于国内众多普通高中的一所并且习惯了大家一起从早到晚在同一间教室里奋斗的感觉，你会惊讶于你的大学同学的日程安排与你的多么的不相干。由此说来，在任何一所大学里，课业量基本都是取决于个人的目标，所以我在这里写的关于北卡教堂山的学习也许只代表我自己的经历。北卡的毕业要求是120学分，在我没有带任何学分进大学的情况下应该保证每学期至少五门课。以我这学期的课程举例，为了在上专业课的同时刷GE，本学期我选了数学、统计、心理、英语（文学）、政治、体育六门课。数学和统计课每周各布置三次作业，数学每周考试，对于一个文科生来说着实不是一件很容易的事情。心理学每周两次作业附带至少20页的教科书需要复习；英语文学每周需要读至少300页的阅读，每周考试；政治学每周20至30页的阅读。本学期我一共有11次月考和8篇论文。虽然较之上学期，本学期的每一堂课都是我感兴趣的，但是也造成了由于课业量大无法正常参加很多学校的活动、腾出时间社交、正常去健身、偶尔做饭、发展音乐方面的爱好等。说到底，课业量的多少取决于个人，成绩来自于个人努力，基础的理科只要努力基本拿A没问题，文科看心情。北卡的总体教学质量偏中上，大多数课由教授上，少部分研究生上的课质量也非常好，所有的任课老师、助教都会有办公时间供答疑，回邮件也很频繁，这对于提高成绩来说是非常有帮助的。我甚至遇到过来自中国的助教向我提供了微信以供答疑，极大地方便了我的学习。
如果你最终选择了UNC，请不要对几百人的大课感到害怕。对此，我的策略是每次只坐前三排，认真上好每一节Recitation。这些大课会将学生分为很多组，提供课后Reciation的机会，给予学生自主在课堂上讨论的机会，亦或是解答理科上的难题。Recitation多由助教主持，一般十几人左右，是巩固知识点的良机。
下面来说一说在UNC读书对于成绩不利的一面吧。首先，我们有包括历史、文学、艺术、国际研究、美国文化、体育、1750年之前的历史十几门文科的General Education，需要在毕业之前读完相关的课程，再加上专业课的要求，每个学期的学习也不是很轻松，所以如果觉得文科太多挤占专业课时间的同学们还是慎选UNC吧。（有一种把公立大U强行读成文理的既视感= =）另外，UNC的GPA Deflation问题十分严重，也就是说在我们这里读书GPA会被压得很低，一般好好读书可以拿到B以上，但是A并不是唾手可得的。很多同学经常会发现，在某些课上努力了一学期，却只拿到了B+；好好写一篇Paper，得个B-或者C+也是常有的事情。如果你在UNC读书，请不要期待有很多让你轻松拿A的课。

Question:

How is University of North Carolina - Chapel Hill for MS in CS?
How is the university in general? And how is the Indian community like? And also how are the placements like?
Answer:
I cannot comment on the MS program for CS but I belong to Class of 2013 at Kenan-Flagler Business School at UNC.

I can only address your questions about the Indian community at UNC and what it is like to be a grad student at UNC.

1. Events : UNC Sangam is the Indian students community and most undergraduate students are very active in it. UNC Sangam also organises cultural events such as Holy Moli and Dandiya during Navratris. These events see huge turnout from students of all countries and of course , Americans. Check it out here[1].

2. Indian community on campus : UNC doesn't have as big an Indian community as some of the other public universities, but I never found that to be a problem. If you can get campus housing it will help you keep your costs down. Else Poplar Place and Autumn Woods Carrboro are beautiful and affordable apartment housing communities to flatshare. I lived in Poplar Place with a room-mate , so I can answer questions about Carrboro / Poplar Place / apartment communities in Chapel Hill, if you have any. Get in touch with Sangam before you move there. They might be able to help you with your initial settling in needs. For me, a fellow Indian student who had lived in NC for a long time really helped me get settled in. I had made a google group for India students who were about to join my class and that's how I found her.

3. Chapel Hill is a small and beautiful student town. But Indian grocery stores are located out of the town i.e. 15 -20 mins drive. You should be able to find other Indian friends on campus and carpool with them for your grocery needs. The famous Franklin Street should take care of your Friday nights and weekends, with some great microbreweries / bars[2] that are always buzzing with activity. Chapel Hill is one of the most beautiful places I have ever called home.

4. Being a grad student at UNC : It took me more than 6-9 months to fully settle in. Until then, it was a continuous struggle because of the rigor of the MBA program combined with the anxiety of settling into a new city and culture. I might add here that before moving to the US, I had lived outside India for more than 4 years, but I still experienced culture shock. Having a car will definitely help you feel settled in faster. On campus parking is pretty limited and only available if you carpool. So you might get the opportunity to share some costs with your fellow classmates. The school helps you line up schedules.

Hope this helps a bit. Give a shout out if you need more information.

[1] UNC's South Asian Awareness Organization
[2] The Top 5 Bars in Chapel Hill on Franklin Street
