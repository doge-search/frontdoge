Clemson University

编辑



Founded in 1889, Clemson University consists of six colleges: Agriculture, Forestry and Life Sciences; Architecture, Arts and Humanities; Business and Behavioral Sciences; Engineering and Science; Health and Human Development; and Education. As of 2015, Clemson University enrolled a total of 18,016 undergraduate students for the fall semester and 4,682 graduate students and the student/faculty ratio is 16:1. The cost of in-state tuition and fees is about $13,882 and out-of-state tuition and fees is $32,800. U.S. News & World Report ranks Clemson University tied for 21st among all "national" public universities. Clemson University is classified as a "highest research activity" university.

Question:

What is the most popular major at Clemson University? Why?
Answer:
Most popular is hard to define.

Do you mean the cool kids? I wouldn't know. Bio majors seem to be the most common in sororities while business/agriculture majors populate fraternities.

Are you looking for the most common? For females it's our nursing program. Quite large. For males, engineering takes the cake. That's me. Over 35% of incoming freshman are general engineering. That number doesn't stay that high because...you know...it's hard, but it's definitely the largest major on campus.

Question:

What are the best reasons to attend Clemson University?
Answer:
I am a recent grad and can speak from my experience at Clemson. Many of Clemson's accolades are listed here Rankings and Brags, but my reasons and experiences are as follows.

Academically, Clemson is a top 25 public university and was ranked 21st in the most recent rankings by U.S. News and World Report. Many of the classes beyond the general classes are pretty small and will often include a TA or Student Instructor that recently passed the class with an A who is there to help.

Beyond academics, my experience at Clemson gave me a sense of belonging and family. The Clemson family is something that the university emphasizes while you're there, but in my experience it is real. Everyone seems to get along and have each others backs.

It is also easy to find your niche. Do you like sports or outdoor activities? There are clubs for just about every sport imaginable that compete in intercollegiate tournaments and events or there are just intramural sports that you and your friends can sign up for and compete against other Clemson students. That is just an example I use because I played club volleyball.

There are tons of organizations that range from Sports, Political Activism, Religious, Professional, to Performing Arts and that's just naming a few of the upper level categories as each of those has multiple organizations under each.

The one thing you should be warned about though is that Clemson is a small town. There is not a lot going on as far as nice restaurants or things to do around town. Even though in the couple of years since I graduated new businesses have been opened. You will have to make the trip to Greenville or Anderson for any real shopping or any real events.

That said, I loved my time at Clemson and if I had to make a decision on what school to attend again I would pick Clemson every time.

P.S. Football gamedays in Clemson are amazing and the small city of Clemson becomes the third largest city in the state on gamedays when there is a home game.

P.P.S. Become friends with someone who has a boat as the lake is practically on campus.
